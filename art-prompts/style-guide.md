# Art Style Guide — Survive the 70s

## Target look
Painted illustration, sun-bleached, slightly faded. Richard Scarry meets
Disco Elysium meets a Polaroid that's been in a drawer for 45 years.

**Palette:** harvest gold, avocado green, burnt sienna, mustard yellow,
warm browns, soft sky blue. Saturation pulled DOWN. Film grain.

**Composition:** wide locked-camera scenes (point-and-click style),
slight wide-angle distortion, characters small in the frame so the
environment does the storytelling.

## Reusable prompt template (Midjourney / Flux / SDXL)
```
[SUBJECT], 1979 American suburb, painted illustration style,
sun-bleached colors, harvest gold and avocado green palette,
soft film grain, slight wide-angle composition, locked camera,
late afternoon golden hour light, wistful nostalgic mood,
detailed environment, no text, no logos, no people in the foreground
unless specified, --ar 16:9 --style raw --stylize 100
```

## Consistency hacks
- Pick ONE generator and stick with it for all backgrounds (mixing
  Midjourney + Flux = different-game-feeling).
- Reuse a seed where possible.
- Generate 4 variants per scene, pick one, save the exact prompt + seed
  in this file for re-runs.

## Forbidden in art
- Any modern objects (smartphones, flat-screens, LED anything).
- Logos of real brands.
- People drawn realistically — keep them painted/illustrative so we
  don't fall into uncanny territory.
