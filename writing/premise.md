# Survive the 70s — Premise (LOCKED v1)

> This file is the canonical story foundation. If anything you write contradicts this doc, change the work — not the premise. Premise edits require explicit sign-off from Mark.

---

## Logline

A modern teenager wakes up in 1976 in the body of Danny Marek — a 14-year-old secret genius who built a body-swap machine, planned a quick "get rich off the future" trip, and got both of them stranded when the machine broke. To get home, the kid has to fake being Danny, rebuild the machine from 1976 parts, and along the way pull off the long con Danny started: a 50-year compound-interest score that makes them both rich in 2027.

---

## The Two Characters

### Danny Marek (the body the player wakes up in)

- 14, lives at 1437 Linden Drive, Old Bay County, Maryland.
- **The load-bearing trait: Danny is a stealth genius.** Reads college physics books hidden inside *Mad Magazine*. Could be skipping grades but deliberately doesn't. Maintains C-minus average on purpose.
- Why hide it? *"If they know what I can do, they'll watch me. I can't build what I need to build if they're watching."* Danny figured out at age 9 that being smart in 1976 means adults make decisions FOR you. Being average means they leave you alone.
- Friends and family think Danny is **a quiet, slightly weird kid who's okay at school and likes to tinker in the garage.** They have no idea he built a working body-swap machine in a garage workshop hidden behind the lawnmower.
- This is the load-bearing trait because **it's why future-kid can fumble through Danny's life without immediately blowing his cover.** Tommy, Mom, the bully — they all expect Danny to occasionally say weird stuff and not know things. *"Danny being Danny."*
- Danny is also a **planner**. He thinks four moves ahead. He's been preparing the Book for months. He's hidden notes everywhere. He thought of (almost) everything. The comedy comes from the few things he *didn't* anticipate.

### Future-Kid (the player)

- 14, lives in the present (2027) in some American suburb. **We deliberately keep this vague** — no name for hometown, no specific school, no family details. The player can project. He's a kid. He has Wi-Fi and a phone and has never used a payphone.
- Knows the year 1976 happened in broad strokes (Bicentennial, *Rocky*, *Star Wars* drops in '77, Carter wins in November). Knows zero local Maryland trivia. Has never been to the Mid-Atlantic.
- Was contacted by Danny via [TBD — see "How the Swap Happened" below]. Agreed to a swap because **Danny pitched it as easy money.** They split the haul 50/50.
- He is **NOT a genius.** He's a regular kid. The puzzles are hard for him because they're hard for *us*. He has to figure out things like "how do you make a phone call without a cell phone" and "what's a fuse box."
- He has a **name the player picks at game start** (default suggestion: maybe just don't bother, refer to him as "the kid" in dialogue). TBD.

## How the Swap Happened (the prologue)

Brief opening sequence, ~5 minutes of gameplay:

1. **Title card: 2027.** Player is in his bedroom. Phone buzzes. Cryptic DM from a Reddit account named u/Danny76: *"You said you'd do it. Tonight's the night. Touch the thing under your bed at midnight. Don't tell anyone. — D."*
2. Player choice: ignore / look under bed. (Game forces you to look under bed eventually.)
3. Under the bed: **a small wooden box that has been there as long as the player can remember.** Player has always known it was there but never opened it. Inside: a quartz crystal, a coil of copper wire, and a yellowed note in 14-year-old handwriting: *"Touch the crystal at midnight. We're switching for the weekend. Trust me. You'll come back rich. — Danny, 1976."*
4. Player touches crystal. Smash cut.
5. **Title card: 1976. Linden Drive, Old Bay County, Maryland.** Player wakes up in Danny's body. Donna Summer on the clock radio. Wood-paneled room. KISS BLAZE poster.
6. The Book is on the desk. Open to page 1. *"If you're reading this, the swap worked. Read carefully. We have until Sunday night."*

**Note:** The wooden box has been there the player's whole life because Danny put it there in 1976, then it sat under various beds (in Danny's family home, eventually inherited or sold and re-inherited, eventually under the player's bed). Danny picked the player specifically — we don't fully explain how (acausal? Quantum entanglement of the crystal? *Some hand-wavy thing.*) The premise is "it works." We don't justify it. *Hot Tub Time Machine* doesn't explain the hot tub.

## What Goes Wrong

- The swap was supposed to be a **weekend.** Friday night → Sunday night. Danny would teach the player his cover stories, the player would experience 1976 for a weekend, then they swap back. Danny would come home with two days of future knowledge worth millions.
- **It's now Monday morning.** Danny's machine in the garage is **gone.** Mom's note on the fridge: *"Cleaned out the garage Saturday. Threw out all that junk you'd been hoarding. Time to grow up, mister."*
- Danny did not anticipate this. Page 47 of the Book has his "If Something Goes Wrong" section, which is mostly blank. The last line: *"...I really hope you're not reading this. If you are, we are in real trouble."*
- **They are stuck.** The player has to rebuild the machine. Danny is stuck in 2027 navigating Wi-Fi and Uber and TikTok.

## The Three Acts

### Act 1 — "Be Danny" (~45-60 min)

- Wake up. Read the Book. Realize the situation.
- Survive a day in Danny's life without blowing cover: breakfast with Mom & Dad, the walk to school with Tommy, sit through math class, lunch, walk home.
- Discover the machine is GONE.
- Find more of the Book's hidden pages around the house.
- Get the parts list. Realize the scope of the problem.
- **Goal of Act 1: orientation + a believable Danny mask + the parts list.**

### Act 2 — "The Parts Hunt" (~2-2.5 hours)

- Hunt down **7 machine parts** across Old Bay County.
- Each part = a small puzzle arc, ~15-25 min each.
- Around the **midpoint of Act 2** (after maybe 3 parts collected), the player discovers the **Book is quantum-paired** — they can write to Danny and he writes back. (Specifically: writing with a specific pen Danny mentions, on a specific page, in a specific way. Or simpler: under a full moon. We'll pick something.)
- **Now communication opens.** Danny in 2027 has had time to think and research. He sends through **the long-con favors list** — 4-5 small tasks that don't help with the machine but will set them up rich in 2027.
- The player can choose to do them or skip them (skipping = poorer ending, doing them = Lou Dorchen ending).
- **Goal of Act 2: 7 parts gathered + 4-5 favors completed + relationship between the two kids deepens through Book exchanges.**

### Act 3 — "Build It & Send It" (~60-90 min)

- Assemble the machine in the garage under the new tarp.
- Realize one final part is missing / they got one wrong / they need a specific broadcast frequency from a specific TV moment to power it.
- Final puzzle: timing the activation to a specific 1976 broadcast event (TBD — Bicentennial fireworks? A specific *Six Million Dollar Man* episode?).
- **The swap.** Player wakes up in 2027. World feels normal.
- **The Lambo scene.** 65-year-old billionaire Danny shows up. Pays out the long con. Credits.
- **Goal of Act 3: tension, build, swap, payoff.**

The romance is not a side plot — it is the **emotional engine** of the game. The machine-rebuild is the body; the love story is the soul.

### Iwona

- 15, just arrived from Poland with her parents.
- Eccentric. Ballroom dancer. Incredibly smart. Beautiful. Heavy accent, speaks little English at the start.
- The accent is intoxicating to Mark. He's instantly hooked. He's also shy and convinced she's out of his league (she's older, she's beautiful, she's brilliant).
- **Specifics (Mark to provide):** personal mannerisms, signature objects, a phrase she mangles, a thing she loves, a thing she hates, her dance style. Mark is the source of truth.

### Why This Solves the Cover-Blowing Problem

Mark-in-Danny's-body can be fully himself around Iwona because **Iwona has no baseline for Danny.** She just got off the boat. She doesn't know how Danny laughs, what his voice cadence is, what he likes. With every other character (Tommy, Mom, Donny), Mark is performing Danny. With Iwona, he gets to be **Mark.** She's the only safe space for him in 1976 — and that happens to be the place he falls in love. That's not just romance. That's catharsis.

### Ending Twist — The Iwona-in-Sylvia Swap

The ending originally locked: Mark wakes up in 2027, 65-year-old billionaire Danny shows up in a Lamborghini, pays out the long con.

**NEW addition (LOCKED v1):**

Mark asks Danny: *"Did you destroy the machine for good in the past so this never happens again?"*

Danny: *"I did. But it happened again."*

It turns out the woman Danny met and fell in love with in 2027 (before the original swap back) is **Sylvia — Iwona's granddaughter.** Also 16, also beautiful, also bewitching. Danny did the same thing Mark did: fell for a girl in the wrong time.

After the original swap back to 1976, Danny lived his life. Decades later, Sylvia and Iwona swap. Iwona's consciousness arrives in 2027 in Sylvia's 16-year-old body. **Mark's Iwona is here, now, with a driver's license.**

The bootstrap paradox: if Iwona-the-young (mind) is now in Sylvia's body in 2027, then someone else's mind must have been in young Iwona's body in 1976. Did Sylvia go back? Did Sylvia grow up to BE the grandmother she descended from? **Closed time loop. Sylvia is her own grandmother.**

Mark's head explodes trying to figure it out.

Danny: *"Don't blow up your brain trying to figure it out. You have a Lamborghini and your Iwona is here now and has a driver's license. How about you two go for a drive."*

Cut to black.

## Thematic Pillars (LOCKED)

Two load-bearing themes the whole game serves:

### Pillar 1 — Soul, Not Body

Mark fell in love with Iwona in 1976. Iwona fell in love with Mark-in-Danny's-body. At the ending, Iwona returns in Sylvia's body. **Neither lover is in their original body when they finally reunite.** They have to accept that the soul is what mattered, not the meat.

This is the *Cyrano / Lake House / Your Name* button. Earned through the entire game's body-swap mechanics. Never preached — just present.

### Pillar 2 — Shared Otherness (Fish-Out-of-Water Squared)

**Both lovers are fish-out-of-water.** Mark from 2027. Iwona from Poland. Shared otherness is the foundation of their intimacy.

- Mark can't tell anyone he's from 2027. Iwona can't fully *be understood* even when she tries. Both are translating constantly. Both are lonely in a crowd.
- Comedy from the same wellspring, opposite ends: Mark doesn't know what a fuse box is because he's from the future. Iwona doesn't know what a fuse box is because she's from Poland. They figure it out together. Tommy thinks they're both idiots.
- They recognize each other before they have words for it. Iwona watches "Danny" mess up something Danny should know and her face flickers — *"I know that face. I've made that face."* That's the moment, long before the kiss.
- The language thing becomes intimacy currency. She teaches him Polish words. He teaches her American slang — some of which is 2027 slang, not 1976, and lands wrong. They build a shared vocabulary nobody else on the block speaks. Their relationship is literally its own dialect.

**Endgame inversion:** When Iwona shows up in Sylvia's body in 2027, **she's the fish-out-of-water now and Mark is home.** The roles invert. She doesn't know what Wi-Fi is. She doesn't know what a smartphone does. Mark — who spent a whole game being lost in her world — gets to be the one helping her. *"Okay, see this glowing rectangle? Don't be scared of it. I'll teach you."*

The whole game has been Mark learning her world. The ending is Iwona learning his. The love story is **symmetric across time.**

## The Tone of the Two-Kid Relationship

This matters because it's the emotional spine.

- **Start:** Player is annoyed. Danny put them here. Danny lied about the weekend. *"This sucks."*
- **Mid-Act 2:** Book opens. First exchanges are functional, then snarky, then genuinely friendly. Danny apologizes. Player softens.
- **Late-Act 2:** They start joking. Danny shares stuff he never wrote in the Book — what it's actually like to be him, why he hides his intelligence, his loneliness. Player shares what 2027 is like — Wi-Fi, social media, the climate, AI. They are the only person each other can be fully honest with.
- **Act 3:** Genuine friendship. They're partners. The final swap is bittersweet — they'll never talk again after this.
- **Ending:** 65-year-old Danny shows up at the player's door. **He remembers.** They have one final conversation. *"You were the best friend I ever had, kid. I told my wife about you. She thinks I made you up."*

## Hint System (LOCKED)

Diegetic. The Book itself. Four tiers per puzzle, player chooses to escalate:

| Tier | Tone | What it gives |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Nudge | Vague | Frames the problem |
| 2 — Direction | Pointed | Tells you *where* to look or *who* to talk to |
| 3 — Method | Specific | Tells you *how* to do it |
| 4 — Solution | Just tells you | Step-by-step |

Each tier escalation Danny writes a snarkier note. No mechanical penalty. Pure social pressure.
