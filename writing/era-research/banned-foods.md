# Banned & Restricted Foods, Substances, and Chemicals — 1970s Era

**Status:** v0.1 draft — built from Mark's research dump (banned foods + everyday banned substances) plus two specific call-outs (Pop Rocks, candy cigarettes). Sibling to `banned-toys.md`. Source spine for ambient world detail, in-game collectibles, dialog jokes, and the secondary "everything in this decade was trying to kill you" comedy throughline.

**Verification key:**
- ✅ REAL — verified historical fact, can use as-is
- 🔶 WRONG ERA — period off, swap or cut
- ⚠️ CAVEAT — real but with nuance worth noting
- 🎭 FICTIONALIZED — parody-1976 version we invent (real brand exists, we rename)

**In-game role tags:**
- **AMBIENT** — appears in scenery / kitchen / dialog. World-building only, not collectible.
- **CONSUMABLE** — Mark can eat/drink/use it. Has a soft-health or comic effect.
- **TRADE** — kid valuables / barter item.
- **JOKE** — sole purpose is comedy beat or Mark-VO observation.
- **HAZARD** — actively dangerous in a quest context.
- **LORE** — referenced via TV/radio/newspaper/conversation only.

## SECTION 1 — Food additives & artificial ingredients

### 1. Cyclamate (artificial sweetener) ✅ REAL
- **Banned:** ~1969–1970 (U.S.). So by 1976 it's already off the shelves.
- **Was in:** diet sodas, "sugar-free" everything.
- **In-game:** LORE / AMBIENT. The diet-soda aisle has a faded "NEW! Now with Saccharin!" shelf-talker because cyclamate just got pulled. Adults complain it doesn't taste right anymore.
- **Comedy:** Mark VO: *"In 2026 we don't trust artificial sweeteners. In 1976 they're banning their THIRD one."*

### 2. Red Dye No. 2 ✅ REAL
- **Banned:** January 1976 (U.S.). **This is current event during the game.**
- **Was in:** M&Ms (the red ones got pulled), candies, cereals, frosting, hot dogs.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT + JOKE. **Major beat:** The red M&M is GONE from packages in 1976 specifically because of this. Mark notices. Real candy bowl in the Marek kitchen — no red ones.
- **Quest hook:** A kid (Bobby?) is obsessed with finding old "pre-ban" red M&Ms. Could be a trade target.
- **Mark VO:** *"You guys just... stopped having red candy. Like, the color. Just gone."*

### 3. Safrole (sassafras / root beer flavoring) ✅ REAL
- **Banned:** 1960 (U.S. FDA) — but homemade sassafras tea was still common in 1976 because farmers and grandmothers don't read the Federal Register.
- **Was in:** real root beer, sassafras tea, gumbo file.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT. Grandma Maggie (Kowalski) makes "the old kind of root beer" from her own sassafras tree. Mark drinks it. *"This tastes... illegal. This tastes federally illegal."*

### 4. Brominated Vegetable Oil (BVO) ⚠️ CAVEAT
- **Status in 1976:** Still legal and in EVERY citrus soda. Not banned until 2024 in U.S.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT. Mountain Dew (or our parody equivalent) is basically rocket fuel.
- **Joke:** *"This soda is illegal in the future. Also delicious. Both things are true."*

### 5. Saccharin ⚠️ CAVEAT
- **Status in 1976:** Still legal. FDA tried to ban it in 1977 (post-game), public revolt, kept it with warning labels through the 90s.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT. The "Tab" / diet-cola aisle.
- **Joke setup for later:** Mark casually mentions saccharin is fine and an adult corrects him: *"Causes cancer in rats. They're trying to ban it."* Sets up the eternal artificial-sweetener panic cycle.

### 6. Aminotriazole (cranberry herbicide) ✅ REAL
- **Pulled:** 1959 "Great Cranberry Scare." By 1976 it's a *parents-still-tell-the-story* event.
- **In-game:** LORE only. Thanksgiving-flashback joke if we ever do a holiday scene.

## SECTION 3 — Pesticides & food production

### 9. DDT ✅ REAL
- **Banned:** 1972 (U.S.). 1976 = 4 years post-ban but still in the food chain, in people, in the soil, in birds' eggshells.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT + LORE. The Marek garage has an old DDT sprayer Mr. Marek still uses on the lawn because *"the new stuff doesn't work as good."* This is real-life behavior — people kept their stockpiles.
- **Joke:** Mark spots the canister. *"That's banned." Mr. Marek: "That's the GOOD stuff."*

## SECTION 4 — Specifically called-out by Mark (these MUST be in the game)

### 10. Candy Cigarettes ✅ REAL — **REQUIRED**
- **Status in 1976:** Wildly popular. Sold at every drugstore, corner store, gas station. **Not banned in the U.S. until the 2000s in some states** — never fully federally banned, but heavily restricted today.
- **Real brands:** Round-the-Clock, Stick-O-Pep, Lucky Lights (parody of Lucky Strike), Marlboro candy (yes really).
- **In-game:** **CONSUMABLE + TRADE + JOKE.** Multiple uses:
  - Mark can BUY them. They're 5¢ at the corner store.
  - They double as cover for real cigarettes if Mark is carrying them ("they're candy, Mom, look")
  - They show the *socialization-to-smoke* mechanic in a single object. Kids practice holding them. Kids "smoke" them at the playground. The teaching surface writes itself.
  - **Mark VO first time he sees them:** *"They sold us cigarettes. As CANDY. The candy was SHAPED LIKE CIGARETTES. To... teach us. To want them. I'm not making this up. None of this is a bit."*
- 🎭 **Parody-1976 name suggestion:** "Lucky Lites" or "Round-the-Block Sticks" — TBD in fake-brand pass.

### 11. Pop Rocks ✅ REAL — **REQUIRED**
- **Launched:** 1975 (General Foods). So 1976 = peak novelty year. Perfect timing.
- **The "Mikey died" urban legend:** ✅ REAL legend. Mikey (the kid from the Life cereal commercials, "He likes it! Mikey likes it!" — real ad starting 1972) was rumored to have died from eating Pop Rocks and drinking Coke at the same time. **The kid did not die.** The actor (John Gilchrist) is still alive. But every 1976 kid *believed it cold.*
- **In-game:** **CONSUMABLE + JOKE + ambient kid-belief-system.**
  - Mark can buy Pop Rocks at the corner store.
  - **The Mikey rumor is a real recurring kid-NPC dialog topic.** Bobby is *certain* Mikey died. He has *the details.* He heard it from his cousin.
  - Mark, from 2026, knows for a fact this is bullshit. He can either correct kids (lose a tiny social beat — "what would you know") or LEAN IN (gain rep — "yeah man, exploded his stomach, blood everywhere"). **Tiny dialog choice with the player.**
  - Mark VO: *"In 2026 we have screens that confirm or deny anything in 4 seconds. In 1976 you have... whatever Bobby's cousin told him. And that's the news."*
- 🎭 **Parody-1976 name:** keep "Pop Rocks" or coin "Crater Crystals" — TBD.

## SECTION 6 — Environmental & industrial chemicals (everyday exposure)

### 13. Leaded gasoline ✅ REAL — **HIGH USE**
- **Status 1976:** Phase-out beginning (1973+), but **regular leaded is still everywhere and most cars require it.** Unleaded pumps were the *new* thing.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT + HAZARD + JOKE.
  - Every gas station has BOTH pumps. Regular (leaded) and Unleaded.
  - **The go-kart engine requires gasoline** — siphoning gas from a parked car (a real 70s kid move) means Mark is putting *leaded gas in his mouth.*
  - **Real-world reference for the modern audience:** Mark VO: *"Lead. Just lead. In the air. In our blood. Average blood lead level of a kid in 1976 is — and this is real — five times the level a 2026 doctor would hospitalize me for."*
  - The leaded-gas backdrop is a quiet engine of the "everyone is lightly poisoned all the time" comedy.

### 14. Lead-based paint ✅ REAL
- **Banned:** 1978 (U.S.). So in 1976 = still legal, still on every old house.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT. Old houses on the block (1920s-era) have peeling paint. Kids pick at it. **A neighborhood toddler is famously known for eating paint chips.** This is the kind of joke that lands hard for Gen X (real memory) and harder for Gen Z (genuine disbelief).
- **Soft-health beat:** if Mark hides behind a peeling porch post for too long during a stealth bit, he comes out slightly dumber for the next dialog. (Optional — calibrate.)

### 15. Asbestos ✅ REAL
- **Status 1976:** Regulation just starting (EPA bans for some uses 1973-78). Still in every school ceiling, every brake pad, every popcorn ceiling.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT + LORE. The Marek basement has classic 1970s asbestos pipe wrap. Mr. Marek is doing weekend home projects in a cloud of white dust and is *fine with it.* "Just dust."
- **Mark VO:** *"That dust is a class-1 carcinogen. He is making sandwiches in it."*

### 16. PCBs (TSCA 1976) ✅ REAL
- **Status:** The Toxic Substances Control Act passed October 1976 = **mid-game current event.** Could land on a TV news segment for ambient newsreel layer.
- **In-game:** LORE only. Ambient TV news mention if we want to date a scene precisely.

### 17. CFCs (aerosol sprays) ✅ REAL
- **Status 1976:** Concern growing, full ban 1978. Every can of hairspray, every deodorant, every Pam cooking spray, every paint can in 1976 is loaded with CFCs.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT + JOKE. Mrs. Marek's hair routine involves a *cloud.* Mark gags. *"Mom — Mom, the entire ozone layer — we have to talk about Australia in like 20 years —"*

### 18. Mercury (thermometers, batteries, fillings) ✅ REAL
- **Status 1976:** Glass mercury thermometers in every medicine cabinet, mercury fillings standard at every dentist, mercury batteries in toys and watches.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT + HAZARD. **Major comedy beat:** Mark breaks a thermometer. Mr. or Mrs. Marek's reaction is *casual cleanup with a paper towel.* In 2026 this triggers a hazmat response. In 1976 you push the silver beads around with your finger because they're *fun.*
- **HAZARD use:** a quest may require mercury for the machine. Real mercury can be sourced from old thermometers, old batteries, old tilt switches — all things in a 1976 garage. This is a CRAFT input.

### 19. Chloroform (water byproduct, household solvent) ✅ REAL
- **In-game:** LORE only unless a quest needs a solvent.

### 20. Synthetic dyes (Red #4, Violet #1, etc.) ✅ REAL
- **Status 1976:** Wave of bans through the decade.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT. Folds into Red Dye No. 2 entry (#2 above). Same joke.

### 21. Secondhand smoke (everywhere) ⚠️ NOT BANNED IN 1976
- **Status:** Smoking is allowed in: airplanes (smoking section in row 22 of a 50-row plane), offices, hospitals (yes), restaurants, grocery stores, doctors' waiting rooms, the back of the family station wagon with the windows up.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT — **constant.** Every interior has at least one character smoking. The Marek house has overflowing ashtrays. The grocery store has them at every register.
- **Mark VO recurring beat:** Every new interior, casually: *"Of course she's smoking. She's a pediatrician. Of course."*
- **This is one of the easiest two-audience wins in the game.** Gen X: "yeah." Gen Z: "WAIT WHAT."

## SECTION 7 — Bonus from Mark's notes that fit here

### 22. Drinking from the garden hose ✅ REAL
- Not technically banned but contains lead solder, leached plastics, dirt, plus whatever was in the spigot last.
- **In-game:** Mark's free hydration source while scavenging. Possibly a tiny health-debuff if used too much in a day. Mark VO: *"This is what dehydration tastes like in the 2026. It tastes like rubber and dirt and that's WATER, that's the WATER."*

### 23. Drinking-age beer in some states ✅ REAL
- **1976 context:** Drinking age was 18 in many U.S. states until the National Minimum Drinking Age Act of 1984 forced 21 nationwide.
- **In-game:** LORE. Explains why a high schooler buying beer at the corner store is *not even shocking* to anyone in 1976.

### 24. Smoking in the car with windows up (kids in back) ⚠️ COVERED IN #21
- Specifically called out because it's such a perfect family-scene set piece. The Marek station wagon, three kids in the back, both parents smoking, windows cracked an inch. This is its own moment.

### 25. Drunk driving as a tolerated norm ⚠️ NOT BANNED but socially routine in 1976
- DUI laws existed but were weakly enforced. The phrase "one for the road" was *literal* — bars handed you a beer to go.
- **In-game:** AMBIENT + JOKE. A neighbor staggering home from the bar on a Friday night is a *scene*, not a crisis.

## SECTION 8 — The Game's "Everything Was Poisonous" Throughline

This catalog supports a deeper running joke than just gags. The cumulative effect of Mark living through:

- The lead in the gas
- The lead in the paint
- The asbestos in the basement
- The mercury in the thermometer
- The DDT residue on the lawn
- The CFCs in mom's hair
- The Red Dye in the candy (now banned)
- The Pop Rocks (rumored killer)
- The candy cigarettes (training device)
- The unrestrained ride in the back of the truck
- The secondhand smoke in every interior

…is a **comedy of accumulation.** Each one is a small "oh god" moment. Together, Mark is realizing his entire body chemistry is being rewritten by a decade that thought lead was *fine.*

**This is the foundation for one of Mark's best big speeches** (location TBD — maybe Act 3 around the Tear opening): *the 1970s kid generation is a clinical-trial control group nobody got consent from, and that group is now running for President in 2026.*
