# The World of Survive the 70s

> The fake Maryland the game is set in. **Old Bay County, MD** — a
> Baltimore-shaped fake county. Real geography compressed and renamed so
> places that were a 90-minute drive in real life can be a short ride in
> the game.

## The State: Maryland
Real Maryland, real-ish in shape. We just invent a new county between
Baltimore County and Anne Arundel and call it ours.

## The Setting: Old Bay County, MD
Population ~80,000 in 1979. Suburban sprawl northwest of the **Crab Cove**
(our fake Inner Harbor). Split-levels, ranchers, a creek behind every
third house, woods that go back farther than they should, and exactly one
of every kind of business — one mall, one arcade, one drive-in, one
liquor store with a guy out front.

## Stomper's Rides (Ocean Side boardwalk)

> Our fake Trimper's. The single most important destination location in the
> game.

**What it is:** A boardwalk amusement park. Family-owned, slightly tired,
mostly amazing. Open since the 1920s. Each ride has a hand-painted sign
that's been touched up too many times.

**Headline ride: The Avalanche** — the Himalaya-style ride. Cars on a
circular track that swoop up and down over a fake mountain. Disco music
blasted at concert-cleric volume from cabinet speakers the size of
refrigerators. When the operator runs it *backward* at golden hour, with
Donna Summer's "Hot Stuff" playing, *that* is the most 1979 thing that
will ever happen to you.

**Other rides:**
- **The Wild Mouse** — wooden coaster, all jolts, no grace
- **The Tilt-O-Hurl** — what it sounds like
- **The Tunnel of Love** — for the older kids; you ride it ironically
  with whoever you dragged along and immediately regret it
- **Bumper Cars** — duh
- **Whack-A-Crab** — fake whack-a-mole with crabs (Old Bay County joke)

**The arcade attached to Stomper's:** Skee-ball, Galaga, change machine
that eats your money, the prize counter where 10,000 tickets gets you a
plastic comb.
