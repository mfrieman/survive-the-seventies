# F is for Family — 70s Culture Idea Mine

**Purpose:** Inspiration bank for *Survive the 70s*. F is for Family (Netflix, 2015–21) is set 1973–74
in fictional Rustvale (Rust Belt). It is dense with exaggerated-but-grounded 70s culture we can ADAPT
(not copy) into quests, mini-games, findable items, characters, and set dressing.

**Usage rule:** This is a *motif* mine, not a copy source. Take the era-texture and comedic situations,
re-skin into our own Burnsville/Oceanside cast and machine plot. Never lift names/dialogue/plot verbatim.
Everything below is an adaptation seed, not a finished design.

---

## A. 70s CULTURE TEXTURE (set dressing + "70s Notebook" entries)
Things the show leans on that instantly read as authentic 1970s — great for the 70s Era Notebook UI,
background props, and "kid from the future doesn't know how this works" comedy beats.

- **The brand-new COLOR TV as a status object** (S1E1). A color set is a big-deal purchase; neighbors
  come over to watch the big match. Magnets wreck the picture. → TV as hero prop + screen-repair beat.
- **Antenna / rabbit ears / "adjust the picture"** ritual — no remote, get up to change 3 channels.
- **Payphones, party lines, no answering machines** — receiving a call at a payphone (already in our design).
- **Smoking everywhere** — on planes, in offices, hospitals, around kids.
- **Drive-in theater** (S3E2) — date spot, sneak-in-the-trunk gag, speaker-on-the-window.
- **The shopping MALL as a social hub** (S1E3) — Orange Julius / food court energy, mall fountain.
- **Catholic church / altar boy life** (S1E6) — confession, Father Pat, guilt comedy.
- **Airport / airline glamour + labor strife** — Mohican Airways, ramp workers, strike threat.
- **Plastics & Tupperware-style home goods** — "Plast-a-Ware," housewife invention/focus-group culture.
- **CB radio / radio DJ culture** — Vic the cool radio guy.
- **Hockey / football corporate seats / boxing on TV** — blue-collar sports culture.
- **"Scared Straight" programs, Catholic Military School** — 70s discipline-of-kids tropes.
- **Lava lamps, wood paneling, shag carpet, avocado/harvest-gold appliances, fondue** — interior palette.
- **Muscle cars, station wagons, jets, joyrides** — vehicle culture.
- **Bicentennial run-up (1976)** — flags, patriotic kitsch (ours is 1976 = peak Bicentennial; mine this hard).
- **Latchkey kids roaming free till dark** — the whole "kids out unsupervised" premise that powers a kid-detective game.

## D. THE "BOX 16" MYSTERY — closest structural match to our game
Season 5's spine: Big Bill, the dying abusive grandfather, gasps "Box 16." Frank spends the season hunting
what that means — finds a **KEY stamped with the number 16**, chases lockboxes, calls relatives for info,
eventually finds recordings (audio tapes) the old man left, and finally gets CLOSURE by talking to one of
the father's **old friends** who explains the real meaning.

Why this matters to us:
- It's the **exact shape of our Act 1 detective premise**: a cryptic legacy (our Box of Leftovers / Danny's
  binder by Buster's grave) + a hunt that requires talking to elders/old friends to decode it.
- **A numbered KEY → a numbered BOX** is a clean, satisfying physical puzzle primitive we can steal wholesale.
- **Audio recordings left by the dead** as the payoff = mirrors our EVP/Reverb spiritual-comms idea (currently
  PARKED) and Danny's recorded instructions. Strong reinforcement that "messages from someone gone" is a
  resonant 70s-grounded mechanic.
- The emotional engine — *decoding a flawed elder's last message* — is exactly our Danny/future-self thread.

## G. R-RATED MATERIAL — adaptation/tone-down guide
The show is R-rated/raunchy. Target rating for our game: **PG-13 leaning mature** (Stranger Things /
Goonies-with-an-edge): kids swear a little, adults are messily real, death/loss present, nothing graphic.
**Core principle: the show's edge = (1) explicit content [sex/gore/hard language] + (2) adult emotional
darkness [abuse/regret/dysfunction/mortality]. We want #2, NOT #1.** Toning down usually = keep the dark
THEME, remove the graphic DEPICTION — often more resonant, not less.

### ✅ Easy to tone down — high value
- **Profanity-as-comedy** (Frank's volcanic, creative tirades, the "F is for…" conceit): keep the cadence
  + comedic *threat* ("I'll put you through that wall!"), swap the vocabulary. Cartoonish over-the-top
  threats are funny at any rating. Free-est win.
- **Teen sexual awkwardness** (Kevin's erection/public-pool humiliation, S2E6/S3E3): dial to "puberty
  mortification" — implied, not shown. Keeps the laugh.
- **Stoner/slacker teen** (Kevin's constant pot use): tone to "burnout older kid" — glassy, unreliable,
  raids the fridge; "what's that smell" haze without depicting use (or lean mild per chosen rating).
- **Raunchy garage-band ambitions**: keep the band, soften lyrics/name jokes.

### ⚠️ Usable with real modification — strong DRAMATIC material
- **Charming-but-abusive new neighbor (Chet) + wife poisons him** (S3): gold for MYSTERY if defanged —
  keep "too-friendly adult hiding something wrong" + a NON-lethal comeuppance. The kid slowly realizing an
  adult isn't what he seems = exactly our suspicious-adult tension, minus the violence.
- **Abusive grandfather (Big Bill) + Frank's childhood trauma** (S4–S5): keep "flawed elder with a buried
  secret/regret," drop on-screen cruelty. Emotional payoff survives; abuse needn't be graphic. (Reinforces
  our Danny/flawed-elder spine.)
- **Kid near-drowning trauma** (S3E7): usable as a character's quiet backstory fear, never shown.
- **Maureen watches an undertaker dismember a body** (S5E1): tone to "creepy funeral home the kids dare each
  other to enter" — spooky not gory. Pairs with our occult/amber layer.

### 🚫 Skip or heavily abstract
- **Workplace sexual harassment of Sue** (S2): keep only as background "men don't listen to the smart woman
  inventor" — strengthens our fabricator-NPC arc without depicting harassment.
- **Carl & Red become porn directors** (S5E5): skip, or abstract to "two laid-off guys with a sketchy 'film
  business'" the kid is warned away from.
- **Graphic violence** (S2E10 domestic-terror bombing; decapitation work accident; mob arson S5): skip gore.
  We already use a machine FIRE as an abstracted plot device — keep that level.
- **Smoking around kids / on planes**: authentic; keep as background haze, don't foreground.
