# That '70s Show — 70s Culture & Quest-Loop Idea Mine

**Purpose:** Inspiration bank for *Survive the 70s*. That '70s Show (Fox, 1998–2006) is set **1976–1979
in fictional Point Place, Wisconsin** — our EXACT starting year. Built around broke suburban teens who
scheme, scavenge, borrow cars, sneak into places, and chase small goals = basically our gameplay verb list.

**Usage rule:** Motif mine, not a copy source. Take era-texture + comedic situation TYPES, re-skin into our
own Burnsville/Oceanside cast + machine plot. Never lift names/dialogue/plot verbatim. The Wikipedia episode
list is mostly Nielsen tables with thin per-episode plots; this doc is built from the show's well-known
recurring structures (far more useful than 200 one-line summaries). All seeds are adaptation starting points.

---

## A. WHY THIS SHOW MAPS TO OUR LOOP (better than F is for Family)
- Same era (1976+), same Rust-Belt-ish suburban setting, same latchkey-teen freedom.
- The kids' entire life IS a quest loop: regroup at base → leave to acquire/scheme/sneak → return.
- Low-stakes-scavenge comedy is exactly our Act 1–2 tone (detective → scavenge/craft).

## B. RECURRING ENGINES = OUR MECHANICS
- **The Basement HQ.** The show lives in one home-base room the gang returns to between adventures.
  → Direct match to our BASEMENT WORKSHOP hub. Leave-do-a-thing-return-to-plan structure.
- **The Vista Cruiser** (hand-me-down station wagon = freedom + breakdowns + the key to going anywhere).
  → Our "need a vehicle/adult to reach a location" gate, esp. Act 3 Oceanside. Borrowed/unreliable car as
  recurring obstacle AND reward.
- **Scheming to get gated things** (beer, fake IDs, into 18+ venues). → Reskins to ACQUIRE-A-GATED-COMPONENT
  quests: forge a note, befriend the clerk, stage a distraction, trade a favor.
- **Part-time jobs as locations + resource taps:** dept-store stockboy, RECORD STORE / PHOTO HUT, modeling,
  radio-DJ gig. Each = a location + a resource the kid can earn or "liberate."
  → The PHOTO HUT is basically our Act 3 FOTO HUT, near-verbatim. Records/electronics = part sources.
- **Concert road trips** (chase tickets to a big show, then travel). → scavenge-for-tickets → travel-unlocks
  multi-step quest. Classic earn-money → buy-coveted-thing → adventure-opens.
- **Parties at the house while parents are away.** → a "host/prep an event" quest: gather chairs, snacks,
  the record player, keep it from going wrong (timer/stealth layer).

## C. CHARACTER ARCHETYPES → NPC FUNCTIONS (re-skin, don't copy)
- **Smooth, dim, vain pretty-boy** (Kelso) → comic-relief ally who breaks things / provides the distraction.
- **Deadpan conspiracy-minded burnout** (Hyde) → INFO-BROKER / black-market NPC; knows where to get the
  un-gettable thing; distrusts authority. (Pairs with our Smokey-type sources.)
- **Foreign exchange student of vague origin** (Fez) → outsider NPC; misunderstandings = comedy; wants
  specific trade goods (candy, etc.) as payment → a barter NPC.
- **Gruff WWII-vet dad, "foot in your ass" threats** (Red) → AUTHORITY-GATE NPC; blocks → respects → helps.
  DIRECT parallel to our STAN. Mine his "earn his respect to unlock the garage/tools" dynamic.
- **Ditzy, oblivious, cheerful mom** (Kitty) → warm NPC who feeds you + accidentally drops key info.
- **Slick used-car/appliance salesman** (Bob) → MERCHANT / haggle NPC.
- **Feminist girl-next-door + radio DJ** (Donna) → capable peer ally; airwaves = rumor/clue delivery system.

## D. ERA-TEXTURE & SET-DRESSING (70s Notebook entries + findables)
- **The "Circle"** (group huddle, spinning camera, woozy logic): won't depict drug use, but the VISUAL GAG
  of a group huddle with loopy reasoning is adaptable comedy framing.
- **The WATER TOWER** ("POINT PLACE" painting prank): perfect MISCHIEF MINI-GAME + town landmark.
- **CB radios, 8-tracks, vinyl/record-store culture, transistor radios** → electronics/part sources + flavor.
- **Mood rings, pet rocks, lava lamps, TV dinners, fondue, shag/wood-paneling** → findables + interior palette.
- **Disco-vs-rock tribal war; KISS / Led Zeppelin / Todd Rundgren fandom** → faction/identity flavor; concert
  arc fuel.
- **Streaking; drive-in; the Hub (teen arcade hangout); roller rink** → ready-made LOCATIONS w/ mini-games.
- **1979 GAS SHORTAGE / oil-crisis lines** → great late-game obstacle (can't just drive; ration/queue puzzle).
- **Grounded-by-Dad / sneak-out** → consequence loop if the kid is caught doing mischief.

## E. QUEST SEEDS (adapt into our economy)
1. **Water-Tower Tag**: pull off a landmark prank without getting caught → stealth/timing mini-game; town
   reputation reward.
2. **Concert Ticket Hunt**: earn/scavenge cash → buy tickets → the trip unlocks a new area/part. (Mirrors our
   earn→buy→travel spine; good Act 2→3 bridge.)
3. **The Borrowed Car**: convince/earn the use of an adult's unreliable car to reach a far location; it breaks
   down en route → roadside mini-quest. (Act 3 Oceanside transport.)
4. **Sneak Into the [Gated Venue]**: forge a note / make a fake / create a distraction to get past an
   age/authority gate guarding a component.
5. **Photo Hut Shift**: work or sweet-talk the photo-hut clerk → access film/chemicals/a developed clue photo.
   (Direct Foto Hut tie — pairs with CP17 Act 3 painting-photo puzzles.)
6. **Record-Store Favor Chain**: the clerk wants a rare record; find it → he hands over a speaker/amp/electronics
   part for the machine.
7. **House Party Prep**: parents away — gather supplies, set up, manage chaos before they return (timer/stealth).
   Reward: an older kid leaves behind something useful.
8. **Earn Red's Respect**: a gruff vet-dad NPC won't open his garage/tools until the kid proves competence via
   a chore-chain. (Stan unlock mechanic.)
9. **Barter with the Outsider**: the exchange-student NPC trades a needed item only for specific candy/goods →
   a small fetch-to-barter loop.
10. **Gas-Line Gauntlet** (late game): the oil shortage blocks easy travel — solve via ration coupons, a siphon
    scheme, or a bike workaround. Late-Act obstacle.

## F. TONE NOTE
That '70s Show is much TAMER than F is for Family (network sitcom): innuendo, teen drinking, implied (never
shown) drug use, mild language. This is actually CLOSE to our PG-13-leaning-mature target — mine it freely.
Its strength = warm ensemble + low-stakes scheme comedy + dead-on era texture. Best veins for us, ranked:
(1) the basement-HQ + return loop, (2) the borrowed-car travel gate, (3) part-time-jobs-as-locations (Photo
Hut!), (4) the archetype roster (esp. Red→Stan, Hyde→info-broker), (5) era-texture findables + the water-tower
mischief mini-game.
