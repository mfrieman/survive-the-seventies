# The Tear System — Stabilization, Costs, Endings

> **Status:** STAGED v0.1 (NOT LOCKED — Mark wants to live with it before committing)
> **Sibling to:** `components/backbone.md`, `components/money-economy.md`
> **Purpose:** Define what the Tear is, how it escalates, how the player stabilizes it, and how it shapes the ending.

---

## 1. What the Tear is (in-fiction)

Danny's body-swap machine was supposed to be a quick zap and back. Instead it **broke during the swap**, leaving a **rip in the spacetime fabric** between 1976 and 2026 — the **Tear**. The Tear is what's allowing all the impossible things:

- Mark Cole's consciousness lives in Danny's 1976 body.
- The Book communicates with Mark — Danny on the other side writing forward in time.
- Comms 2 (the CB radio reveal beat) — actual voices crossing.
- Iwona begins to "remember" things that haven't happened yet.

**Critically: the Tear is getting worse on its own.** Even if Mark did nothing, the rip widens over time. Every install of a machine component pulls more energy through it and widens it faster. Endgame is *sealing* the Tear before it tears the world.

---

## 2. The two halves of "Tear cost"

We discussed unifying or splitting. Decision: **unified, but with two visible faces.**

- **Physics face.** Installing components widens the Tear. Predictable, escalating, on the machine spine roadmap.
- **Metaphysics face.** Moral choices (mostly small) flavor the Tear. They don't compound into hard penalties — they show up as Mark VO weight, world atmosphere, and ending texture.

**One system, two surfaces.** The player feels the same gauge but understands it from two angles.

## 4. Stabilization tiers (REVISED — Mark Test compliant)

The original design had "burn a treasured item" as the recurring stabilization cost. **Pulled back** because it triggers hoarding anxiety in casual / older-novice players who fear losing things they may need later (the Mark Test — see `gameplay-loop.md`).

**New design:**

### Tier 1 — TIME (recurring, used 2-3 times across the game)

When the Tear hits a moderate threshold (after C/F, after K/G), the next install is **blocked** until the Tear settles.

- **Cost:** Mark must **wait 1-2 streetlight transitions.** *Not* full days. A streetlight transition is "day ends, bedroom phase, sleep, next morning."
- **Player experience:** The Book says: *"Wait. Let it settle before you tug at it again."*
- **What can the player do during the wait:**
  - Anything *except* install the next component
  - Free to earn money, build relationships, do side quests, explore, eat free dinner with the family
  - Read new Book entries (Danny writes more during the lull — narrative bonus)
  - Bedroom phase remains untimed during waits
- **No items destroyed. No money spent. No relationships harmed.** Pure time cost.
- **Day-budget impact:** ~2-3 streetlight transitions total across all Tier-1 stabilizations. Fits inside the 35-day summer window without crowding side content.

**Why this works:**
- Casual players never lose anything they collected.
- Pressure lands on players who *only* speedrun the machine — they're forced to engage with side content or sit there.
- Pressure is mild on players who already do side content — they don't even notice.
- Pacing-natural: every stabilization beat is a chance to read Book entries and slow down for atmosphere.

### Tier 2 — A SINGLE SCRIPTED RELATIONSHIP FAVOR (one time, Act 2)

When the Tear hits a major threshold (after K install), Mark needs someone to **cover for him** so he can spend a critical night working on the machine without interruption.

- **Cost:** Mark asks ONE of three people to lie for him. Player chooses who. **One-time scripted moment.**
  - **Mickey Karras** — lies to Mark's parents. Cost: Mickey's now owed something, plays out lightly in Act 3.
  - **Bobby** — lies for big brother. Cost: Bobby loses a piece of innocence, gets a tiny shadow he didn't have before. Mark VO is heaviest here.
  - **Iwona** — lies to her parents about being with Mark. Cost: Mark dragged her closer to the mystery she shouldn't know about. Smallest mechanical impact, biggest moral.
- **Effect on ending:** Shapes one specific detail in the Act 3 ending scene.
- **No item destroyed. No cash spent.** Pure relational choice.

### Tier 3 — THE MEMORY BURN (one time, scripted, Act 3 climax)

When the Tear is catastrophic, with Component A about to install, the Book makes a request **of Mark Cole specifically** (not Danny):

> *"One more thing. I need to draw on what makes you, you. Pick something you can lose."*

Mark is **alone in his bedroom** at this moment. **Iwona is not there** — per the locked premise that she does not know about the machine. The scene is intimate, quiet, between Mark and the Book.

The player chooses from a menu of **4-5 memories** the game has surfaced across the playthrough (each one mentioned in Mark VO at some point in Acts 1-2):

- The smell of his wife's hair *(if Iwona romance was deep — landed as a callback)*
- His apartment number in 2026
- The words on his grandfather's gravestone
- The name of his cat
- His favorite song
- (5th slot reserved — TBD, maybe surfaces per playthrough based on what Mark talked about most)

**Mechanically:**
- Player picks one. The Book inks it in (a small page of the Book is now scribbled). Mark can no longer recall it. The ending sequence references the forgotten thing — an NPC asks about the cat in the dance scene, Mark blanks, recovers gracefully.
- **One-time event.** The player is told this is coming (Book foreshadows two days prior with: *"I will need to ask something of you."*). No surprise dread. No hoarding anxiety.
- **The happy ending happens regardless of choice.** What changes is *the flavor of the final scene* — which memory absence colors the ending.

**Why this works:**
- One bold mechanic without polluting the whole game with sacrifice dread.
- Becomes the *signature moment* players talk about online (*"I burned the cat's name. What did you burn?"*) — replay/community fuel.
- Doesn't gate the happy ending.
- Honors the premise (Iwona doesn't know).
- Has the Disco Elysium-shaped impact you wanted.

## 5. The ending shape — three textures, all happy

**Locked rule:** Danny, Iwona, Sylvia, and the dog are all in the 2026/27 dance-scene ending. No bad ending. No sacrifice ending. The happy ending is the canon spine.

**Varies:** The *weather* of the ending, plus a handful of small details.

### Three ending textures

**Texture 1 — "Quiet Victory" (intimate)**
Just Mark and Iwona on the floor. The dance is private, no one else in frame. Sylvia and the dog implied off-screen. Mark VO is quietest. Soft. *"We got it. Both of us. Quietly."*

**Texture 2 — "Reunion" (full)**
Everyone in the room. Sylvia, dog, maybe Bobby (older), maybe Dennis (older) showing up to the anniversary. Conversation, multiple beats. The dance is the centerpiece but it's a *family* scene. *"Everyone came."*

**Texture 3 — "Echo" (wistful, the door is open)**
Mark and Iwona dancing. The machine in the garage **still hums.** Camera holds on it for a beat. Mark VO: *"We didn't fix it. We sealed it. There's a difference. And the difference is — we could go back."* Last line opens the door without forcing a sequel.

### What determines which texture you get

A small handful of cumulative choices across the game, weighted lightly so no single decision flips the ending:

| Factor | Pushes toward |
|---|---|
| Iwona romance depth (Comms 2 mask-drop choices, dance scene engagement) | Quiet Victory or Reunion |
| Bobby relationship state (covered for him, included him in beats) | Reunion |
| Halloran relationship state (apprenticeship done, his story heard) | Reunion (with his photo on the wall: *"Lost him in '04"*) |
| Dennis bond (Friday allowance arc, the $5 handoff) | Reunion |
| Cumulative Tear damage at climax (the gentler your installs, the cleaner the seal) | Quiet Victory |
| Did Danny "get rich"? (Mark's mid-game choices about leaving notes for Danny about future events) | Affects whether 2026 Mark is wealthy or comfortable. Background detail. |
| The memory burn choice | Surfaces in a specific line/moment in the ending |
| Tier 2 relationship-favor choice | Surfaces in a specific Act 3 detail (who calls, who shows up, what gets resolved) |

**Players who replay don't "fix" anything — they just see different colors of the same victory.**

**Stretch goal — 4th texture:** If we have appetite later, a 4th "Echo+" with one specific dialogue tree open that reveals the machine still works AND that Mark "remembers" he visited 1976. This would be the deepest cut for completionists. Optional.

## 6. Diegetic signals for system limits (no UI popups)

Per Mark's request — never break the vibe with explicit text. Soft signals to teach the player how the Tear system works:

| Situation | Diegetic signal |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 wait triggered | Book entry: *"Wait. Let it settle."* + Mark VO: *"Right. Okay. Tomorrow."* |
| Player tries to install during wait | Machine refuses (visual: spark, brief crackle, no progress). Mark steps back. *"...not yet."* |
| Wait period ending | Book entry next morning: *"Now."* Single word. |
| Approaching a Tier 2 moment | World gets stranger — a TV show interrupts itself, a song on the radio skips a line and resumes |
| Tier 3 (memory burn) about to be needed | Book entry days in advance: *"I will need to ask something of you. Soon."* Foreshadows so the player isn't blindsided |
| Approaching Tier 3 menu | Specific page of the Book is set aside, blank. Player KNOWS something is coming. |

## 7. What we explicitly ruled out (and why)

- **❌ Item-burning recurring stabilization (original Tier 1).** Triggers hoarding anxiety. Failed Mark Test.
- **❌ Money-based stabilization.** Allows grinding past the mechanic. Breaks the economy.
- **❌ Hard "game over" on Tear catastrophe.** No way to lose the game.
- **❌ Branching to "bad endings" with character death / Iwona missing.** The happy ending is canon spine.
- **❌ Iwona at the machine for the memory burn.** Breaks the locked premise that she doesn't know.
- **❌ Visible Tear meter as a numeric stat.** Use the ink-stain-in-the-Book metaphor instead.

---

## 8. What the player sees as a "Tear gauge"

**The ink stain on the Book's cover page.** Starts as a small smudge. With each install, the stain spreads. At catastrophic levels, the stain has overwhelmed the page.

- **Period-appropriate "UI"** — it's literally a hand-drawn metaphor, not a stat bar.
- **Vibey but readable.** The player can glance at the Book and *know* roughly how bad things are without numbers.
- **Memory-burn page is separate** — its own blank space inside the Book that the player can't open until the Book offers it.

## 9. Open questions (Mark to react to before LOCKING)

1. **Is "wait 1-2 streetlight transitions" the right Tier 1 cost?** Could be shorter (just one transition) if even 2 feels too much. Could be longer if 2 feels too soft.
2. **Tier 2 favor — three options OR more?** Three is clean. More risks dilution.
3. **Tier 2 — is the "covers for me" framing too specific?** Could the Tier 2 cost be a different kind of relational ask (e.g., asking Mickey to *introduce* you to someone Mark wouldn't normally meet)? Lean: covers-for-me is the most natural and least complicated.
4. **Memory burn menu — final list of 4-5 items.** Need to write the actual VO lines across Acts 1-2 that *establish* each memory so the player feels the loss when they burn one. Production task.
5. **Should the 2026 wife memory (Iwona's hair smell) be on the menu?** Has poetic resonance (Iwona-in-1976 ↔ Iwona-in-2026) but feels cruel. Lean: yes, but flag with player — Book hints *"this one will hurt."*
6. **The ink-stain-in-the-Book UI** — concept locked, but needs an art-direction note. The Book is the only "UI" the player sees. We should commit to that as a design principle.
7. **Foreshadowing the memory burn — how many days in advance?** Two feels right. One is jarring. Three is melodramatic.
8. **Should there be a single Tier-2 moment OR could there be 2-3 different scripted favors across Act 2?** Lean: one, save the moral weight.
9. **Do alternate ending textures reveal themselves on first playthrough or only on replay?** Lean: first playthrough, with a Bookmark / "save your ending" feature so player can revisit it.
10. **Calendar pressure check** — Are we sure Tier-1 waits eat ≤3 streetlight transitions total? Verify when wiring quests.md.
