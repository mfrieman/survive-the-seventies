## 1. The core concept (v2)

The second pathway is **not** a small painting and **not** something you inhale. It's a **body-sized
ritual platform** — a large flat **board painted by Denise** that the swap-subject **lies down on.**

- Around the board are **sockets/spots for the five stones** (a ring) plus the **amber at the
  center** (resting over the subject's chest/heart) plus **spots for other ritual items.**
- It's the **swap altar.** Path 1 (the electrical machine) *sends the signal*; Path 2 (the platform
  the body rests on, charged by stones + amber) is *what the signal acts upon.* Together they aim
  the swap **at the person lying on the board** — which finally answers Mark's question: *how is
  Danny/Mark the TARGET of the machine?* **He's the target because he's lying on the array.**

| Pathway | Substrate | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| **Signal (Path 1)** | Perfboard / Erector frame (electrical) | The emitter — sends the radiation/signal. **This is what burned.** |
| **Second (Path 2)** | **Denise's painted platform** + stone ring + center amber | The altar — the body lies on it; it focuses the swap onto that body. **Not burnt — just drained.** |

## 2. Everything moves to the BASEMENT

The machine (both pathways), crafting, and storage live in **Danny's basement** — the classic 1970s
**"dungeon basement"** kid domain (wood paneling, drop ceiling, concrete floor, the place parents
don't go). This is the **crafting/storage center** for the whole game.

- Matches prior canon: the **basement workshop** (CP17) and the That-'70s-Show **basement-HQ** motif
  already in the research mine.
- Asset fit: POLYGON interiors (Town/Horror Mansion modular rooms) cover a paneled basement easily.
- Gives the game a **home base** — you descend to build, surface to quest.

## 3. The fire + drain backstory (revised)

- **Only Path 1 burned.** The electrical machine is the charred wreck. (Preserves the locked
  "machine = destroyed from minute one" beat — it's specifically the *Signal* machine.)
- **The platform did NOT burn.** It's intact but **inert.**
- **The five stones are SPENT.** The first soul-transfer burned out their charge. Mark must obtain
  **five fresh stones** at Oceanside. → Payoff: Danny **keeps the spent, dull husks**, and uses them
  as **reference samples** to recognize/match the fresh ones at the five locations Denise's father
  left them. (Great identification/matching gameplay — the husks are inert, but they *teach the
  eye*.)
- **The amber is SPENT too** — and **cannot be reused.** Mark must obtain a **fresh
  amber-with-inclusion.** This is the love-story key (see §4): the new amber ultimately comes from
  **Iwona** (concert kiss-and-gift). **RULED 2026-05-30:** both stones and amber are spent from the
  first swap; both must be replaced fresh.
