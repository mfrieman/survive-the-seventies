# Act 3 — Oceanside, Amber, the Kiss, the Swap

**Genre:** Reset — new place, new gameplay. Vacation-with-a-mission energy.
**Ends on:** Swap fires during chaos of Bicentennial concert. Mark wakes up in 2027.
**Scope:** Oceanside (Old Bay County boardwalk). Old Bay block is offscreen.

---

## Act 3 thesis

> Mark has fallen in love with Iwona and built the machine. Now he has to **leave her** — and the last component is hidden in the one place he's never been.

The emotional arc: **hate → love → must-leave.** Act 3 is the **must-leave** half.

---

## Setup — getting to Oceanside

**Iwona drives Mark to Oceanside.** She is the only person on the block who can/will take him. The trip is the structural reason Act 3 reframes — new location, no Danny-cover-test load (Iwona doesn't know Danny), pure Mark+Iwona time.

**Pretext:** Mark needs to find something at the antique dealer (the science kit) AND something on the beach (amber). He needs Iwona because:
- She has the car / ride access.
- She knows the amber authentication tests (she's Polish).
- He doesn't want to do this without her.

---

## Three things Mark must accomplish in Oceanside

1. **Get the science kit** (from the antique dealer). Procedural Act 3 fetch.
2. **Find amber with insect inclusion** that authenticates as real.
3. **The kiss.** Iwona-side of the love story closes.

## The amber arc  🔒 LOCKED

### The beaches-banter payoff

Iwona on the boardwalk, looking at the seashell-and-souvenir shops: *"On our beaches we can find amber. Versus stupid seashells."* This is the trigger.

Mark: *"Wait — your beaches have amber?"* Then asks about **inclusion**. Iwona shows her necklace. *"This is real. Real amber, real bug, all the way from Poland."*

### The amber hunt

The Oceanside boardwalk has multiple amber stores (just like Polish Baltic beach towns). Mark goes shop-to-shop. **Most amber is fake** (1970s tourist trade).

### The authentication tests  🔒 LOCKED — REAL TECHNIQUES

Iwona knows the three real tests:

1. **Saltwater Float** — Mix 7 tsp salt in 1 cup water. Real amber floats; fake sinks.
2. **UV Blacklight** — Real amber fluoresces under UV.
3. **The Rub Test** — Rub amber on cloth until hot; real amber smells like pine resin, builds static charge.

**Where Iwona finds the props (diegetic):**
- **Saltwater:** The ocean. (Or a cup from the boardwalk trash + ocean water.)
- **UV light:** Pirate's Cove haunted house attraction (blacklight era — felt paintings, fluorescent skeletons).
- **Cloth:** Iwona's own clothing.

**Mark finds amber with insect inclusion.** Tests it. **It's fake.** Devastation.

### The transfer  🔒 LOCKED

Mark doesn't want to ask Iwona for her necklace — it's too precious. He doesn't say a word about needing it.

After the kiss on the beach, **Iwona takes the necklace off and puts it around Mark's neck.** She doesn't know about the machine. She doesn't know why he needs it. She just gives it. *"This is for you."*

**That completes the machine.**

(Mythology cross-ref: This works because Iwona has committed in her heart and soul to passing the necklace down to her future granddaughter — that intention ripples through the amber, anchoring Sylvia for the future Iwona-Sylvia swap. See magic-system docs. **Mark doesn't know any of this** — he just received the gift.)

## The Bicentennial concert (climax setting)  🔒 LOCKED

**July 4, 1976. Boardwalk. Massive open-air concert.**

Lynyrd-Skynyrd-style parody-headliner band. Beer cans shooting beer everywhere. Pot smoking. Fireworks going off **during** the set. Crowd losing its mind. Free as a Bird-style guitar solo peaking.

Mark and Iwona are in the crowd. Splashed with beer. Splashed with champagne. The chaos is total.
