# Act 1 — The Detective Game

**Genre:** Pure detective game — knowledge-as-progression, Outer Wilds model.
**Ends on:** Mark installs enough components to open comms with Danny in 2027. **One-way door — Act 1 genre ends.**
**Scope:** Zone 1 (the Oaks block) + limited Zone 2 (Burnsville Electronics + a few key shops).

---

## Act 1 thesis

> Mark has a box of parts, a receipt, a notebook, and a neighborhood he doesn't recognize. He has to figure out *what he has, who can help, and where to go* before he can talk to Danny in the future.

Until comms opens, **Mark is alone with the mystery.** No help from Danny. No exposition dumps. Pure investigation.

---

## The four knowledge UIs (Mark's progress is tracked here)

1. **Map** — discovers neighborhood Places (one tile at a time).
2. **People** — adds NPCs as Mark meets them: who they are, what they know, what they can do.
3. **70s Notebook** — Mark's running journal of 1976 stuff he's figured out (rotary phone takes change, payphones can be called back, etc.).
4. **Machine Blueprint** — slots for every component. Placeholders fill in as parts are identified and installed.

## Opening scene (Saturday morning — leaving the house)  📝 DRAFT — NEW TODAY

**Setup:** Mark comes down the stairs. The dining room is still smoke-filled. Kay and her friends are still playing cards. Bobby (7, Mark's "brother") is sprawled on the living room floor watching cartoons.

### What Mark sees in the living room

A very large, odd "computer" with a kid lying in front of it streaming some cartoon. Steel antlers come out of the top with pieces of aluminum foil wrapped around the ends.

**The joke:** It's a TV. The antlers are the antenna. The foil is how 1976 kids made reception work.

### The two-choice tutorial (both wrong on purpose)

Player picks one:

- **(A) Go to the "computer" and ask PAL to provide details of this location.**
- **(B) Go to the "computer" and ask PAL to fix the broken machine.**

Both choices are nonsense — **this is the gameplay teach.** PAL doesn't answer (PAL doesn't exist here). The TV doesn't respond to voice. **Player learns: 1976 objects don't work like 2027 objects.**

Mark walks to the TV and starts messing with it (turning dials, fiddling with the antenna).

### Bobby's interruption

**Bobby (yelling):** *"MOM! Danny is messing with my cartoons!"*

### Kay's "Wherever" speech  🔒 LOCKED VERBATIM

Kay comes into the living room with her cigarette, looks stern at Mark:

> *"Stop that. In case you forgot, smart guy — this is Saturday morning, which means Saturday morning cartoons. Unless you go and get us a second TV, the rules stand from when you were a kid. Saturday morning is for Saturday morning cartoons. Used to be for you. Now it's for Bobby."*
>
> *(beat)*
>
> *"Now — Bobby gets the TV until the cartoons end this afternoon. Then he'll go wherever."*
>
> *(beat)*
>
> *"I can tell you three things about this place called 'Wherever':*
>
> *One. You are supposed to be there right now.*
>
> *Two. It is not located in this house.*
>
> *Three. When the first streetlight comes on at the end of the day, you leave Wherever and bring your ass home."*

**Critical:** Kay just established that even Bobby (age 7) is also kicked out to "Wherever" later in the day. This is the 1976-parenting thesis in one speech.

### What this scene accomplishes

- ✅ Tutorial: 1976 objects ≠ 2027 objects. PAL is gone. Player learns by trying.
- ✅ PAL-absence beat lands (payoff for Opening Beat 1).
- ✅ Bobby established (tattletale, owns the TV, also out of the house later).
- ✅ Kay's voice + the 1976-parenting comedy thesis.
- ✅ **The streetlight rule = Act 1 day-clock.** Diegetic time pressure. No HUD.
- ✅ Mark is kicked out of the house with a mission: *go to Wherever.* He has to figure out what 14-year-old Danny would do on a Saturday.
- ✅ **One TV in the whole house.** Kay's *"unless you go and get us another TV"* line confirms it. Mirror-and-inverse of Mark's 2027 bedroom (screens everywhere, all on). Same scarcity thesis, opposite hardware. The TV is a *contested resource* — that's why Bobby owns the morning and Mark can't have it.
- ✅ **The detective game starts now.**

## Day-clock + house-as-hub  📝 DRAFT — DESIGN LEAN

Modeled on **Schedule I** day-clock with adventure-game adaptations.

### The house stays open as a hub

After Mark is pushed out by Kay, **he can return to the house at any time.** The bedroom IS the machine workshop — he needs to come back to install parts, check the blueprint, examine the notebook in context. Closing the house off forces UI work into menus and kills the diegetic feel.

**Family rooms (dining, living room) are soft-gated.** Kay plays cards. Bobby watches TV. Mark walking through earns commentary; lingering risks stat hits or Kay questions. **Bedroom = Mark's. Rest of house = adult territory.** That mirrors how 1976 kids actually felt.

### Daytime — free play

- Time advances when Mark **travels between locations** and **completes scene-beats.** Not a real-time tick.
- Standard adventure-game trick: time is a *cost paid by action*, not constant pressure.
- Examples: Burnsville Electronics visit ≈ 1 chunk. Denise conversation ≈ 1 chunk. Bike ride to Zone 2 ≈ 1 chunk.
- A full day = roughly **5–7 chunks**.

### Streetlight warning (~1 chunk before night)

Visual cue — sky tints orange/purple. Maybe Kay phone call to wherever Mark is. Reminder, not a fail.

### Streetlights on — curfew

Mark **must** be home. If he's not:
- Mom's Patience hit
- Possible quest-lock (some quests fail if Mark missed curfew that day)
- Comedy beat with Kay regardless

### Night — house mode (free time)

Once Mark is home at night, **the house becomes the "free time" space.** Same model as Schedule I.

- **Time does NOT advance** while Mark is in the bedroom.
- Player can install parts, read the notebook, study the blueprint, sort inventory, plan tomorrow.
- Take as long as you want.
- When player chooses **"Sleep"**, one beat passes. Next morning begins.

### Why this works for our game specifically

- **Detective game during the day** (Outer Wilds-style knowledge-gathering under time pressure).
- **Tinkering / synthesis at night** (machine workshop time, no clock).
- Player controls pacing — the day is the pressure, the night is the breather.
- Maps cleanly to 1976 kid reality: outside until dark, then your room is your kingdom.

## Act 1 structure (Mark's design)  📝 DRAFT

### The four things to figure out (tracked across the four UIs)

1. **The neighborhood (Map UI)** — Discover Places. Soft-gated so Zone 2 isn't fully open in Act 1.
2. **The people (People UI)** — Who is who? Who knows what? Who can help with which machine part?
3. **The 1970s (70s Notebook)** — How does this era *work*? Phone, money, transportation, social rules.
4. **The machine (Blueprint UI)** — What parts are needed, what's in the box already, what's missing, what's the build order.

### Soft-gating Zone 2

❓ **Open design question.** Mark's instinct: a **bike** unlocks far locations. But a stubborn player could walk anywhere given enough time.

Options on the table:
- **Knowledge gate:** Mark literally doesn't know where Zone 2 places are until an NPC tells him. (Outer Wilds model.)
- **Mom gate:** Kay's "where are you going?" check — Mark needs a plausible cover story to leave the block.
- **Bike gate:** Bike unlocks reasonable travel times to Zone 2; without it the day-clock burns too fast.

**Clawpilot recommendation:** Stack all three. **Knowledge Gate + Mom Gate + Bike** = soft-linear without feeling like locked doors.

### Compressed early progression

Mark identifies a few parts he *already has in the box*. Quick installs early on — gives the player early wins, establishes the "find part → install → blueprint updates" loop. **By mid-Act-1 the blueprint is partially filled.**

### Act 1 ending — the one-way door  🔒 LOCKED PATTERN

Mark installs enough components to **open comms with Danny in 2027.**

- This is a **point of no return.**
- Detective-game genre ENDS here.
- Comms with Danny is intentionally limited (1-2 messages per act, max).
- Act 2 genre = scavenge / mini-quest / collect / craft / build.

**Comms beat at end of Act 1:** Mark sends *"I'm scared. I don't fit in. Get me home."* Danny replies *"Same. I hate it here too."* — mutual confession. (Anchor — `decisions-log.md`.)
