# Opening — Cold Open to Title Card

**Length target:** ~6–8 minutes of play.
**Ends on:** Title card ***SURVIVE THE 70s***.
**Player sees:** Cutscene-heavy intro, two short interactive moments (TV ask + smoke detector reply), then bedroom mechanics intro.

---

## Naming rule (enforce throughout)

- **Mark** = always the future-kid consciousness. Even when in Danny's body.
- **Danny** = always the 1976 host consciousness. Even when in Mark's body.
- The era tells you the body. Don't write "Mark-as-Danny."

---

## Beat 1 — Saturated 2027  🔒 LOCKED

**~2 min. Cinematic.**

Mark in bed. Hologram screens, scrolling tickers, autoplay videos, notifications stacking. Never quiet. **PAL** (his AI assistant) is the only voice that talks *to* him — smooth, attentive, ever-present. We never see his parents; they're irrelevant in this world.

**Thesis:** Humans only talk through machines now. Mark is alone in a room full of voices.

---

## Beat 2 — The Switch  🔒 LOCKED

**~30 sec. Cinematic.**

Mark wakes dizzy, nauseous. Screens still buzzing. He falls back into bed. Black.

---

## Beat 3 — Wake up as Danny  🔒 LOCKED (verbatim Kay dialogue)

**~3–4 min. Short interactive moment + cinematic.**

Mark opens his eyes. Foreign bedroom. **Smells smoke.** Panics, runs downstairs.

**Dining room:** Kay Marek and her friends at the table, cloud of cigarette smoke, playing cards.

Mark (called Danny by the room — but we still call him Mark in scripts/notes): *"There is smoke."*

Kay: *"I don't know — why did the smoke detector go off?"* — (no, that's Mark's line. Verbatim below.)

### Verbatim Kay smoke-detector exchange — DO NOT REWRITE

> **Mark:** *"There is smoke."*
>
> **Mark (confused):** *"I don't know — why did the smoke detector go off?"*
>
> **Kay:** *"Smoke detector? What the hell do I need a smoke detector for? I can see the smoke. I detect smoke, smart ass. What I need is a kid smart enough to open the fucking window upstairs and get the smoke out."*
>
> *(beat)*
>
> **Kay:** *"We're playing cards. Go do something, Danny."*

**First time he's called "Danny."** Mark doesn't react aloud — confusion only.

Mark goes back upstairs. Shuts the door.

---

## Beat 4 — Bedroom Mechanics Intro  📝 DRAFT

**~2 min. Lightly interactive — first time player clicks objects.**

The fire is out. The room is quiet. Mark sits on the bed to get his head straight. Player gets first chance to **look around** — this is the mechanics teach.

### Things in the room (player can examine, in any order):

1. **The window with the plastic sheet** — taped over an empty frame next to the bed.
   - Mark removes the plastic to vent the smoke.
   - **Plant — no callback yet.** (End-of-Act-2 finale button: Mark sits on the windowsill with a beer.)

2. **The READ ME notebook** — on the desk, handwritten cover, impossible to miss.
   - Click → opens the **Notebook UI** (one of the four knowledge UIs).
   - First page is the letter (below). Subsequent pages reveal as player progresses.

3. **The machine** — on the desk/table. Charred, smoldering, ruined.
   - Click → opens the **Machine Blueprint UI** (one of the four knowledge UIs).
   - All component slots show as **placeholder silhouettes**. Nothing installed.

4. **The box of parts** — under the desk or beside it. Wooden box.
   - Click → opens the **Inventory** with starter parts pre-loaded.
   - Some of these turn out to be machine components (player won't know which yet).

5. **The receipt** — tucked in the box or on the desk.
   - First quest hook. Hints at **Burnsville Electronics** as a destination.

### The READ ME letter  🔒 LOCKED

> **READ ME (SERIOUSLY READ THIS)**
>
> If you're reading this, congratulations — and sorry. You're not dreaming. My name is Danny Marek. I'm 14. I built a machine that swaps two minds across time and you got picked. I tried to time it so you'd sleep through the whole thing.
>
> The rules:
> 1. **It's an hour. ONE HOUR.** Then we switch back automatic. Sit tight.
> 2. **DO NOT TOUCH THE MACHINE.** I mean it. If you mess with it I can't bring us back.
> 3. My mom's name is Kay. If she asks, you're "going to bed early." She won't ask.
> 4. There's a kid named Bobby. He's seven. He's annoying. Ignore him.
>
> Why? I'm going to look up some stock prices and football scores in your year and write them down. When I'm back I get rich. When you wake up in your own bed tomorrow you'll find an envelope under your pillow with your cut. **50/50, I swear on my Bombard Mark IV.**
>
> Sit tight. Don't break anything. Don't tell anyone.
>
> — Danny
>
> *P.S. The window by the bed has a plastic sheet over it. Don't lean on it.*

**The joke:** Danny thinks the missing window is the most dangerous thing in the room. Also: **the machine is already destroyed** — Danny's plan is already broken; Mark just doesn't know it yet.

Mark glances up. **Machine = pile of charred junk.** Realizes the "hour" plan is gone.

## Beat 5 — Year reveal + title card  ❓ OPEN

**~30 sec. Cinematic.**

Three options on the table — pick one before scripting:

- **(a) Implicit:** Visual only via Bicentennial bunting in Kay's dining room downstairs. Player figures it out; Mark doesn't say "1976" aloud.
- **(b) Explicit beat:** Mark sees a calendar / poster / Carter-Ford bumper sticker. Says: *"...nineteen seventy-**six?**"* Smash cut to title.
- **(c) Hold it:** Don't confirm year until early Act 1.

**Clawpilot recommendation:** (b). Gives the opening a button. Title hits *after* he says the year.

---

## Cross-cutting anchors established here (don't re-explain later)

- 🔒 **PAL absence** — Mark will try "PAL, turn on the TV" in Act 1 opening scene. PAL doesn't answer. First time he's truly alone in his own head.
- 🔒 **Plastic-sheet window** — set up here, paid off at end of Act 2 (Mark on windowsill with a beer).
- 🔒 **Machine = destroyed from minute one** — drives the entire game. The "1 hour and we swap back" plan is dead before the player has any input.
- 🔒 **Four knowledge UIs introduced** — Notebook, Machine Blueprint, Inventory, and (implicitly) Map.
  - **Map** opens when Mark first leaves the house in Act 1.
  - **People** UI opens at first meaningful NPC interaction in Act 1.
