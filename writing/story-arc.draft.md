## NAMING CONVENTION (read this first)

**Always refer to the consciousness by name. Never the body.**

- **"Mark"** = always the future-kid (the player). Even when in Danny's 1976 body.
- **"Danny"** = always the 1976 host. Even when in Mark's 2027 body.

After the swap, **Mark is always in 1976** and **Danny is always in 2027.** The era tells you the body — no need to spell it out.

**Drop "Mark as Danny" / "Danny as Mark" entirely.** Just write "Mark" and "Danny."

| ❌ Old | ✅ New |
|---|---|
| "Mark as Danny walks downstairs" | "Mark walks downstairs" |
| "Mark as Danny tells Danny as Mark he hates it" | "Mark tells Danny he hates 1976" |
| "Danny as Mark in the future replies" | "Danny replies from 2027" |

**When we need to specify the body** (rare — only for what *other characters see*), use parentheticals:
- *"Kay sees Danny (Mark) walk in."* — Kay sees the 1976 body; Mark is inside.
- *"Mark catches his reflection — Danny's face stares back."*

**In dialogue, in-game characters use the body's name** (because that's who they think they're talking to):
- Kay says *"Danny, set the table"* → she's talking to Mark.
- A 2027 friend says *"Yo Mark"* → talking to Danny.

This honors **Thematic Pillar 1: Soul Not Body** — the name follows the soul.

## Cross-Cutting Anchors (locked threads — do not violate)

These are creative load-bearing decisions that the acts must honor:

- **PAL** — In 2027, Mark's only "voice" is an AI companion called PAL. Smooth, ever-attentive. Theme: humans only talk through machines now. Mark losing PAL when he wakes up in 1976 is a real psychological loss.
- **Mark Cole** = future-kid consciousness (the player). **Danny Marek** = 1976 host consciousness. 1976 cast says "Danny" to the player; 2027 cast says "Mark" to Danny. Never collapse the two names in writing.
- **Danny acted without Mark's consent.** Danny assumed Mark would either sleep through it or be "smart enough" to handle it. Both assumptions were wrong.
- **The machine is broken from the start.** Mark sees it ruined on the table within the first 4 minutes of the game.
- **Comms cadence (Mark ↔ Danny across time):** LIMITED. At most 1–2 communications per act. The mechanism is TBD (will be designed after the arc locks). Specific comms beats Mark has called out:
  - **Act 1 — first comms:** Mark tells Danny "I hate it here, I want to come back." Danny replies "I hate it here too, I want to come back."
  - **End of Act 2 — final comms of that act:** Danny says: *"I'm getting the idea that you don't want to come back."*
- **Plastic-sheet window (Act 2 finale button):** End of Act 2 closes on Mark balancing a chair on the windowsill, feet hanging out the (sheeted) window, beer in hand, staring into the night. He has fully settled into 1976. **DO NOT spoil or callback before the Act 2 button.**
- **Iwona-heartbeat scene (Act 2):** Mark holds Iwona; it's so quiet he can hear her heart. Direct sonic inversion of the Opening Beat 1 noise. This is the emotional fulcrum of Mark's "I want to stay" turn.
- **July 4th 1976 Bicentennial beach concert (Act 3 climax):** At the Old Bay County equivalent of Ocean City ("Ocean Side" / the boardwalk). Big crazy beach concert with a fake-parody headliner band. Almost certainly: Iwona present, machine arc converges here.
- **Iwona never knows about the swap until the ending.** This is a hard rule. The romance has to land authentically *before* the reveal — meaning Mark has to win her as himself, not as a confession.
- **Novel-grade character work for ~10 main-line characters.** The game operates like a nice novel. No throwaway NPCs in the main cast. Every named main-line character has interior life. Sub-characters (parents-of-friends, smoking card-game ladies, Donny the bully, etc.) get one well-drawn trait each and serve as stereotype-flavor / environment. The ~10 main-line slots (subject to revision):
  1. Mark / Danny (two sides of one character)
  2. Iwona Kowalski
  3. Kay Marek
  4. Dennis Marek
  5. Bobby Marek
  6. Mickey Karras
  7. Athena Karras *(or Gus — pick one)*
  8. Peter Kowalski
  9. Maggie (the senile grandma who sees through people)
  10. One TBD wildcard (possibly Natan, possibly someone new)
- **Era Teaching System** — the 70s teaches itself through gameplay, not cut-scenes. See dedicated section below.
- **Emotional arc shape:**
  - **Act 1:** Mark hates 1976, desperately wants out. Danny (in 2027) hates 2027, also desperately wants out.
  - **Act 2:** Mark transitions hate → love (Iwona dance scene is the pivot). Ends on the window-and-beer button.
  - **Act 3:** Mark realizes he MUST go back. Finishes the machine. Climax at the Bicentennial concert.

## OPENING — v0.1 draft

> Short cut-scene. Get to the game fast. Total ~5–6 minutes.

### Beat 1 — Saturated 2027 (~90 sec, all cinematic)

Mark in bed. Hologram screens floating around him: news tickers, autoplay videos, sports scores, group chats, notifications stacking. **PAL** speaks — smooth, gentle, motherly-but-not — reminding him about tomorrow's calendar, suggesting a sleep playlist, asking if he wants the screens dimmed. Mark mumbles non-answers. PAL is the only voice he hears. We never see his parents. The world is *loud* and *attentive in the wrong way.*

### Beat 2 — The Switch (~20 sec, cinematic)

Mark suddenly dizzy, nauseous. PAL: *"Mark, your vitals are spiking. Should I call—"* He falls back. Screens keep buzzing as everything goes black.

### Beat 3 — Wake up as Danny (~3 min, partial gameplay starts here)

- Mark opens his eyes in a foreign bedroom. **Smells smoke.** Panics.
- Player gets minimal controls — just walk. Runs down the stairs.
- **Kay + the smoking card-game ladies** in the dining room. Smoke-detector exchange (verbatim, Mark's original draft — it's perfect):

> **Mark** *(to Kay)***:** "There is smoke."
>
> **Kay:** "Of course there's smoke, that's why they call it smoking."
>
> *(Kay notices the smoke drifting down the stairwell.)*
>
> **Kay:** "What the hell are you smoking up there?"
>
> **Mark:** "I don't know — why did the smoke detector go off?"
>
> **Kay:** "Smoke detector? What the hell do I need a smoke detector for? I can see the smoke. I detect smoke, smart-ass. What I need is a kid smart enough to open the goddamn window upstairs and let the smoke out. We're playing cards. Go do something, Danny."

- **Note:** Kay calls him "Danny" — that's the first time the player hears the swap-name confusion in dialogue. Kay sees Danny's body. Per our naming convention, the narration still calls him "Mark."
- **Year reveal is environmental, not dialogue.** Behind Kay: Bicentennial bunting, "SPIRIT OF '76" calendar, flag-print pantsuits, a commemorative plate. Player clocks the year. Mark does not say it out loud.
- Walks back upstairs. Closes the bedroom door. The machine is *smoldering, ruined* (no active flames anymore — trimmed per Mark). Pulls the plastic sheet off the broken window (no comment — held for Act 2 callback). Sits on the bed.
- Sees a notebook on the dresser with a HUGE **READ ME** sign.
- Reads the letter (Clawpilot's pitched rewrite, locked):

> **READ ME (SERIOUSLY READ THIS)**
>
> If you're reading this, congratulations — and sorry. You're not dreaming. My name is Danny Marek. I'm 14. I built a machine that swaps two minds across time and you got picked. I tried to time it so you'd sleep through the whole thing.
>
> The rules:
> 1. **It's an hour. ONE HOUR.** Then we switch back automatic. Sit tight.
> 2. **DO NOT TOUCH THE MACHINE.** I mean it. If you mess with it I can't bring us back.
> 3. My mom's name is Kay. If she asks, you're "going to bed early." She won't ask.
> 4. There's a kid named Bobby. He's seven. He's annoying. Ignore him.
>
> Why? I'm going to look up some stock prices and football scores in your year and write them down. When I'm back I get rich. When you wake up in your own bed tomorrow you'll find an envelope under your pillow with your cut. **50/50, I swear on my Bombard Mark IV.**
>
> Sit tight. Don't break anything. Don't tell anyone.
>
> — Danny

- Mark glances up at the table. The machine = pile of charred junk.
- **Title card: SURVIVE THE 70s.**

## ACT 1 — v0.1 draft (~80% solid per Mark, 2026-05-23)

> **Opens:** Immediately after the Opening cliffhanger. Mark is in Danny's bedroom, staring at the ruined machine and the open notebook.
> **Closes:** First comms exchange with Danny ends. Danny's final line: *"You have no idea."* Page goes blank. Black screen. **ACT 2.**
> **Emotional arc:** Mark hates 1976. Danny hates 2027. By the time they communicate at the end of the act, both are confirmed miserable — and stuck with each other.

### Story beats in order

**1. The notebook reveal (gameplay starts, ~5 min)**
- Mark opens the notebook past the READ ME letter. Finds: parts list (machine repair manual) + a hand-drawn map labeled *"If something goes wrong — bury this in the lockbox under Buster's headstone, Old Bay Pet Cemetery. I'll find it."*
- Mark gathers what he can from Danny's room (money, tools, backpack — diegetic inventory tutorial).
- Mark goes downstairs and steps out the front door.

**2. The Block Walkout — fish-out-of-water (gameplay, ~3–5 min)**
- **No cut-scene.** Player gets full controls. Camera pans up as the door opens.
- The block IS the tutorial:
  - Bobby's plywood-and-brick bike ramp halfway built in the yard.
  - A lawn dart stuck in the lawn from yesterday.
  - Two kids ride past on banana-seat bikes, no helmets, one steering with no hands holding a slingshot.
  - Across the street: shirtless dad mowing, cigarette in mouth, bare feet, kid trailing with the rake.
  - Driveway: older teenager waxing a muscle car, 8-track blasting parody-rock through open windows.
  - Background sounds: Bombard Mark IV (parody Big Wheel) scraping concrete, screen door slamming, someone yelling for Donny to get inside for dinner.
  - **What's missing:** No phones in hands. No earbuds. No adults looking at devices. Kids everywhere; adults barely visible.
- **Mark's only VO line during the entire sequence** (subtle, one sentence):
  > *"...where are all the parents?"*
- That's the whole fish-out-of-water beat. *Absence* tells him the era as much as presence. Then he walks up to the first NPC.

**3. The Tutorial NPC ladder — meeting people who already know "Danny"**
- **First NPC: Bobby** (low cover-blow risk). Bobby is the perfect tutorial — small brain, exposition-friendly ("when are you gonna fix my bike? You said yesterday you would!"), annoying-by-design. Establishes the Cover Risk Meter gently: weird Mark behavior just makes Bobby say *"Daaanny you're being weeeeird,"* not raise alarms.
- **Second NPC: Mickey Karras** (higher stakes — peer with deep knowledge of "Danny"). Lives in the Dungeon (garage). First place Mark might find machine parts (electronics, amp gear). Sample tutorial-through-frustration beat:
  > **Mickey:** "Dude, hand me the church key."
  > **Mark:** "...the what?"
  > **Mickey:** "The CHURCH KEY. From the drawer. We do this every day."
  > **Mark:** *(finds bottle opener)* "...isn't a church key—"
  > **Mickey:** "A CHURCH KEY IS A BOTTLE OPENER, MAN."
  > *Player learns: "church key" = bottle opener. Forever remembered.*
- **Third NPC: Kay** (adult — sees through bullshit but is too distracted to care). She's the hardest to fool *in theory* but functionally the safest because she's not looking.
- This is the **difficulty ramp built into the cast** for Act 1.

**4. Parts gathering (gameplay, the bulk of Act 1)**
- Mark uses the notebook's parts list to scavenge Zone 1. Some parts are easy. Some require fooling NPCs. Friends function as quest givers and helpers.
- Parts count for Act 1: **TBD** (pitching 2–3 parts findable in Zone 1 — Mickey's garage, Karras basement, Bobby's stash). Larger parts are saved for Acts 2 (broader town) and 3 (Ocean Side / antique store / harder finds).

**5. Iwona moving in — first sighting (near end of Act 1)**
- Mark and his friends are outside (specific location TBD — bike ride? sidewalk hangout?). A moving van pulls up to the Kowalski house.
- Iwona gets out. Mark stares.
- Friend delivers the line (LOCKED, polished):
  > *"Don't even, dude. That's the new Polish family. She's like sixteen. You're a kid. You're Old Bay trash. She's an actual goddamn princess. Don't embarrass yourself."*
- The "Old Bay trash" self-deprecation lands the regional joke without meanness. Sets up Act 2: she is impossibly out of his league. Makes the eventual romance land harder.
- Mark does not approach her in Act 1. Just sees her. Plants the seed.

**6. The Drop — burying the notebook (gameplay ritual, ~5 min)**
- Mark goes to Old Bay Pet Cemetery. Per the notebook, finds Buster's headstone. There's a small lockbox already buried in the dirt.
- He puts the notebook in the box. Buries it. Walks home.
- **This is the act's commitment moment.** Player has done the ritual that commits to the long con.
- Screen fades.

**7. The Notebook Returns — first comms event (cinematic-light cut-scene, ~3 min)**
- Mark wakes the next morning. Notebook is back on the nightstand. Cover now reads **READ ME AGAIN!**
- He opens it. Danny's handwriting fills new pages.
- **Voiceover** narrates only the highlight lines in Danny's voice — the rest is on the page, not read aloud, so we respect player reading time.
- A diegetic timer is visible: the notebook itself glows softly and the glow is fading. When the glow goes out, the comms window closes. Adds urgency.
- **The exchange (LOCKED template — full draft below):**

> **Danny:** I guess something went wrong because I can't get back. You need to fix the machine. We can write in this notebook and communicate, but only for short windows. *(Mechanism details TBD — flag for machine-rules pass.)*
>
> **Danny:** I have bad news for you. Your world is completely destroyed.
> Everyone's plugged in. No kids playing. Everything is *safety*. I went for a bike ride and got stopped by the cops — no helmet, no knee pads, no wrist pads, no cup. I had to go back to your house and put on a SUIT OF ARMOR just to ride down the block.
>
> *(NOTE: 2027 is NOT destroyed. Danny just sees it as awful and assumes the machine ruined the timeline. He's wrong. Comedy + character truth in the same beat.)*
>
> **Danny:** You need to get me back. I can't build the machine from here — I can't get the parts. It's not gonna be easy for you. It took me TWO YEARS and I had access to stuff I can't get you access to anymore.

> **Mark:** *(player picks from 2–3 dialogue options, all variations of: "Your world is awful too.")* This place is awful. A lawn dart almost went through my foot today.

> **Danny:** I went to one of these "fast food" places to get a milkshake. They gave me a "Shake." No "Milk." I asked what was in it. NOTHING BUT TOXIC CHEMICALS SERVED IN A PETROCHEMICAL TOXIC CUP. And you're criticizing us for a fucking LAWN DART. At least we're outside doing shit.

> *(A few more back-and-forths — exact lines TBD, voice locked. Both kids realize they're stuck with each other. Both pissed.)*

> **Danny:** Look. You hate it there. I hate it here. Get me my parts and we BOTH go home. Don't fuck this up.

> **Mark:** *(player option, frustrated)* "How hard can it be."

> **Danny:** *(final line of Act 1, LOCKED)*
> ***"You have no idea."***

- Page goes blank. Notebook glow fades. **Comms window closes.** Black screen. ***ACT 2.***

## ERA TEACHING SYSTEM (design pillar, applies to all acts)

> The 70s teaches itself through gameplay, not cut-scenes. Players who care can dig in; players who don't still absorb the world subconsciously. We respect player time at every layer.

**Layered system:**

| Layer | Method | Player effort | What we teach |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ambient** | Background NPC animations, environmental detail | Zero | "This world is dangerous and unsupervised" |
| **Diegetic** | Examine objects → Mark VO reacts as a 2027 kid | Optional click | Specific 70s artifacts (cigarettes, payphones, etc) |
| **Conversational** | NPC frustration when Mark fakes 70s knowledge | Forced by quests | 70s vocabulary, social norms (e.g. "church key" = bottle opener) |
| **Collectible** | Scrapbook / lunchbox / Danny's Cool Stuff Drawer | Opt-in completionism | Deep cultural lore, banned toys, era artifacts |
| **Story-critical** | Quest items literally ARE 70s objects (machine parts) | Required | Big-hitter dangerous toys (Gilbert U-238 atomic lab, etc.) |

**Sample examine-VO lines** (Mark reacting to 70s objects):
- **Cigarette vending machine:** *"Wait. A machine. Selling cigarettes. With a pull-knob. Where you put COINS. Where's the ID check? Where's the ID CHECK?"*
- **Pay phone:** *"I don't... I don't have coins. Where am I supposed to get coins. WHY DOES THIS HAVE COINS."*
- **TV with tin foil on rabbit ears:** *"...is this a sculpture? A 1976 sculpture about defeat?"*
- **Metal playground slide in July sun:** *"That's gonna give a kid third-degree burns. That's a CHILD oven. I'm gonna call CPS — wait. There IS no CPS."*

**Inspiration sources:** Disco Elysium (sausage-paragraph examine system), BioShock audio diaries, What Remains of Edith Finch (environment-as-exposition), Red Dead Redemption 2 (background NPCs performing life), Outer Wilds (drop player in, let them figure it out).

**No cut-scenes for any of this** except act-opening cinematics. The 70s teaches itself by being *present* and *interactive*.

**Reference need:** Mark to provide the "70s kid culture" list (lawn darts, deadly metal playgrounds, banned/toxic toys, etc.) — that list seeds both the ambient layer AND the machine-parts quest design.
