# Story Bible — Survive the 70s

> The rewriting target for every AI-drafted line. If a line doesn't fit
> what's below, rewrite it.

## Premise
Saturday, July 17, 1976. The narrator is a 9-year-old in a generic American
suburb. Mom has issued the standard directive: "Go outside. Be home when
the streetlights come on." Five scenes, one day, one ending.

## Narrator voice
- **Adult looking back**, not a kid in real time.
- Dry, affectionate, mildly horrified. Think David Sedaris doing voiceover
  on a home movie.
- Past tense, first person. ("I picked up the lawn dart. This was, of
  course, a weapon.")
- Comedy comes from the gap between *what the kid thinks is happening* and
  *what the adult narrator now understands was happening*.

## Inner voices (max 3 — keep it simple)
1. **Common Sense** — quiet, usually right, usually ignored.
2. **Dumb Kid Bravery** — loud, charismatic, the reason we're here.
3. **Mom (Imagined)** — the voice in your head reciting things Mom has
   already said this week. Triggered by especially bad ideas.

## Tonal rules
- **Punch up or punch sideways, never down.** The joke is on the era and
  the dumb kid (you). Never on a real group of people.
- **No nostalgia worship.** The 70s were genuinely dangerous and weird. The
  humor depends on acknowledging that, not romanticizing it.
- **No real brand names** unless they're so generic they read as period
  detail (e.g., "Big Wheel" is fine, "Coca-Cola" we'd swap for "the cola").
- **Cursing:** PG-13 at most. "Hell" and "damn" sparingly, for emphasis.
- **Stakes are real but cartoon.** You can break bones, you can't actually
  die. Failures are funny, not grim.

## Era detail toolbox (sprinkle, don't dump)
**Hazards:** lawn darts (Jarts), metal slides, BB guns, banana-seat bikes
with no helmets, riding in the way-back of a station wagon, garden hose
water, Pop Rocks + cola panic, secondhand smoke fog, Stranger Danger vans,
homemade Halloween candy, asbestos popcorn ceilings, leaded gasoline fumes,
trampolines with no nets.

**Objects:** rotary phone, CB radio, 8-track, transistor radio, mood ring,
sea monkeys, Stretch Armstrong, Big Wheel, Schwinn Sting-Ray, slip 'n
slide, Easy-Bake Oven, lawn jarts, walkie-talkies that don't work past 30
feet, the Sears catalog.

**Sounds:** screen door slap, ice cream truck (creepy), cicadas, the
clatter of plastic on a tube TV, AM radio, a Big Wheel on concrete.

**Smells (mentioned, not simulated):** chlorine, hot vinyl car seats, lawn
mower exhaust, Coppertone, secondhand smoke.

**Phrases the narrator/Mom uses:**
- "Be home when the streetlights come on."
- "Don't make me come out there."
- "You'll shoot your eye out."
- "We have food at home."
- "Wait 30 minutes after eating or you'll cramp up and drown."
- "Close the door, were you raised in a barn?"

## Do / Don't
| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Make the kid the butt of the joke | Mock the era's marginalized groups |
| Treat danger as comedy | Treat injury as nothing — it should hurt |
| Show Mom as exasperated and loving | Make Mom a villain |
| Use specific period detail | Drown the scene in references |
| Let failure be the funnier outcome | Punish the player for trying things |

## Skill check writing pattern
Every check has THREE outcomes written:
1. **Crit success** — the kid genuinely pulls it off; narrator marvels.
2. **Pass** — works, but barely.
3. **Fail** — something goes wrong; this is usually the funniest line.

Fail should never be a dead end. It costs a stat or opens a new dumber path.

## Forbidden
- Anything sexual involving the kid.
- Detailed real violence (a BB gun welt is fine; gore is not).
- Real political figures or events as punchlines.
- Modern internet humor voice ("bestie," "no cap," etc.). The narrator is
  an adult in 2026 but doesn't talk like Twitter.
