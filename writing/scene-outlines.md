Saturday, July 14, 1979. One day. Five scenes. ~15 minutes of play.
---

## Scene 1 — Kitchen (the briefing)
**Location:** Linoleum, harvest gold appliances, secondhand smoke haze
from Dad's morning cigarette, a Pop-Tart half-burned in the toaster.

**Beats:**
- Mom delivers the marching orders ("be home when the streetlights come on").
- Dad behind the Sun-paper, grunts.
- Allowance negotiation — failed or successful changes how much cash
  you have for Scene 3 (the 7-Eleven).
- Choose: argue with Mom (loses Mom's Patience, gains Cool Points
  internally) or eat it and go (saves Patience, narrator winces at
  young-you).

**Skill check:** Common Sense vs Dumb Kid Bravery — talk Mom into an
extra dollar. Fail = she remembers you were "fresh" all weekend.

---

## Scene 2 — Garage (the loadout)
**Location:** Concrete floor, oil stain, Dad's pegboard, a Schwinn
Varsity leaning, a tackle box of stupid ideas.

**Loadout: pick 2 of 5:**
1. **Walkie-talkie** (works at 30 feet on a good day) — unlocks comms
   gag in Scene 4.
2. **The lighter** (a Bic with a sticker on it that you found, you did
   not buy) — gates the m-80 joke and one other thing in Scene 4.
3. **Three bottle rockets** — chaos option.
4. **A folded-up Playboy page** (just the article, swear to god) —
   social currency with older kids in Scene 3.
5. **Five bucks you've been saving** — gates the 7-Eleven shopping
   list and the buy-us-beer-guy attempt.

**Skill check:** none. Choices are the gameplay.

---

## Scene 3 — The 7-Eleven
**Location:** The 7-Eleven. Slurpee machine grinding. The older kids
behind the dumpster smoking Marlboros. Karen behind the counter (she
is 15, she has a job, she is out of your league, you are going to
talk to her anyway).

**Branching beats:**
- **Slurpee math:** Coke + cherry + a little Mountain Dew. The
  combination is called something stupid like a "Suicide." You buy one
  or you don't.
- **The Shoplifting Dare:** Whoever's with you dares you to pocket a
  pack of Big League Chew. (TBD — Danny doesn't have a single best
  friend; recast for specific tag-along when scene is written.) Skill
  check: Common Sense vs Dumb Kid Bravery.
  - Crit success: you walk out clean. Cool Points up.
  - Pass: you get out with it, sweating.
  - Fail: the cashier (NOT Karen) catches you. Either talked-down
    (Karen vouches for you, romance flag++) or your dad gets a call.
- **The Buy-Us-Beer Guy:** Some skeevy older guy in a Members Only
  jacket leaning on a Pinto. Requires the $5. He'll get a six-pack of
  Schlitz for $2 markup. Skill check: do you trust him?
  - Crit success: he comes back with the beer. Cue Scene 5 payoff.
  - Pass: he comes back with the beer, also keeps a can.
  - Fail: he takes the $5 and never comes out of the store. You
    learn a lesson about adults. Narrator notes the price of
    tuition.
- **Talk to Karen:** zero stat gates. Three dialogue options ranging
  from "smooth" (you think) to "I have made a horrible mistake."
  Failure is the funny one. Sets up a Scene 5 phone call.

## Scene 4 — The Creek / The Woods / The Abandoned House
**Location:** Behind the houses on Linden Drive. A creek 6 feet wide,
8 feet deep. Woods that go back further than you think. The Abandoned
House (it's just empty, a family moved out two years ago, but at 14
it is OBVIOUSLY haunted).

**Branching beats (pick 2 of 3):**
- **Jump the Creek on the Schwinn.** Build a ramp out of plywood and
  a cinder block. Skill check (Dumb Kid Bravery, hard).
  - Crit: legendary. Cool Points cap out.
  - Pass: clear it, knock the wind out of yourself on the landing.
  - Fail: into the creek. Bike chain off. Walking home wet. Mom's
    Patience down.
- **Light an m-80 in the storm drain.** Requires the lighter.
  - Crit: hilarious echo, no one heard.
  - Pass: hilarious echo, somebody's mom heard.
  - Fail: lit it too close, ringing in your ear for two days. Bones
    Intact down.
- **The Abandoned House dare.** Go inside, leave a mark on the
  basement wall, get out. Atmospheric. Maybe the kid you're with
  chickens out and you go alone, maybe vice versa, maybe you both
  go. (Friend TBD when this scene is written.)
  - Crit: you find an actual treasure (a stack of Playboys from
    1974). Currency for later.
  - Pass: you do it. Adrenaline.
  - Fail: a raccoon. Or worse: the next-door neighbor saw you go in
    and is calling Mom.

**Optional substance moment:** If a tag-along is with you (TBD which
kid for this scene) and you've made enough "bad" choices, he produces
a half-joint his brother left. Skill check to participate or pass.
Either choice has a funny outcome. Participate = the world tilts
comedically for the rest of the scene; pass = he thinks you're a
narc, peer-dip but Common Sense up.

---

## Scene 5 — Streetlights / Home (the verdict)
**Location:** Riding home in the long blue-purple summer dusk.
Streetlights humming on one by one. Cicadas. Your block.

**Beats:**
- **Race the streetlights.** Time-pressure mini-beat (Ren'Py supports
  this via timed choices). Make it home with the lights or after.
- **The dinner-table verdict.** All your day's stats are tallied.
  Mom asks "How was your day, hon?" and the dialogue branches by your
  state:
  - Wet from the creek? Bandage on your knee? Smell like a joint?
    Somebody's mom called? Karen called? Cop called?
  - Narrator delivers the closing voiceover that wraps the day with
    1979 vs 2026 perspective.
- **One closing line per ending variant.** Maybe 4-6 possible last
  lines depending on how the day went.

---

## What makes the demo "done"
- All 5 scenes playable end-to-end.
- At least 3 different endings based on player choices.
- At least one full-on laugh-out-loud moment per scene (the bar for
  "concept proven").
- Backgrounds + portraits consistent enough to feel like one game.
- Music + one or two key SFX (screen door, Bic lighter, bike chain,
  rotary phone dial).
