## 3. What Player KNOWS (Initial Signposts)

Plant these BEFORE the player engages with the quest:

| Signpost | Location / Trigger | Player-type targeted |
|---|---|---|
| Playboy Tony sits on his front lawn shirtless reading Playboys | Visible from street, ambient | All players |
| Trying to talk Tony into giving/earning a Playboy fails | Dialogue with Tony | Average player tries first |
| Tony's trash cans have NO Playboys | Examining trash, multiple attempts | Average player tries next |
| Elga (Tony's mom) is sweet, scattered, talks about the messy house | Brief meetup, ambient dialogue | All players |
| Boy Scout Friend is doing the **Paper Drive** — flyer mentions "newspapers AND magazines" | Flyer pinned to neighborhood telephone pole; friend in uniform handing them out | Sharp player connects immediately |

**The "a-ha" trigger:** the word **MAGAZINES** on the Paper Drive flyer. That's the single piece of information that unlocks the chain. Make it visually obvious on the flyer (bold, capitalized).

---

## 4. What Player FIGURES OUT (The Puzzle)

> *"Oh — I need to borrow the Boy Scout uniform, pretend to be doing the Paper Drive, knock on Elga's door when Tony's not on the lawn, and she'll happily clean out her old papers — including Tony's Playboys."*

Logical chain:

1. I want a Playboy magazine
2. Tony has them but won't give them up
3. Tony doesn't throw them out
4. Elga (his mom) has a messy house with old papers
5. The Boy Scout Paper Drive collects newspapers AND magazines
6. If I show up at Elga's door as a Boy Scout, she'll donate the old papers
7. The donated papers will include Tony's Playboys (because they're piled in the house)
8. I need a Boy Scout uniform → my friend has one → he'll lend it if I [trade item]
9. I need to time it so Tony isn't on the lawn (he'd recognize me / stop the donation)
10. Knock → collect → SCORE

## 5. Failure Feedback (Diegetic, Not Punishing)

Failures are CONTENT, not walls. Each failure teaches AND entertains.

| Wrong attempt | NPC / world response | Hint embedded |
|---|---|---|
| Ask Tony for a Playboy | *"Beat it, kid. These are collectors' items."* | Confirms Tony has them but won't give |
| Offer Tony money for a Playboy | *"You couldn't afford one of these. Now scram."* | Confirms cash won't work either |
| Check Tony's trash (attempt 1) | Empty bottles, no Playboys | Encourages player to wait/return |
| Check Tony's trash (attempts 2-4) | Increasingly mundane garbage, no Playboys | Builds doubt |
| Check Tony's trash (attempt 5) | **Tony catches Mark:** *"You're after my Playboys, ain't ya? I don't THROW 'EM OUT, kid. What kind of animal do you think I am?"* | Hard-confirms trash is a dead end + adds character beat. **Disables further trash-check responses.** |
| Knock on Elga's door while Tony IS on the lawn | Tony intercepts: *"Ma! Don't open the door for these kids! They want my Playboys!"* Elga calls back: *"Tony, sweetheart, I'm not opening anything!"* Door doesn't open. | Teaches the time-gate (need Tony absent) |
| Knock on Elga's door WITHOUT Boy Scout uniform | Elga is friendly but: *"Oh, what a sweet boy. No, I don't need anything today. Tell your mother I said hello!"* | Teaches uniform is required |
| Ask the Boy Scout Friend for the uniform without trade item | *"It's my UNIFORM, man. My mom would kill me. What do you got?"* | Teaches trade is required |

## 6. Signpost Escalation Ladder

This is the rescue system. Player who doesn't figure it out gets progressively more obvious hints over in-game time. Sharp players never see these.

| Day after quest available | Source | Hint content | Subtlety |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day +0 | Flyer on telephone pole | "BOY SCOUT PAPER DRIVE — Newspapers & **MAGAZINES** — Saturday" | Subtle (sharp player gets it now) |
| Day +0 | Friend handing out flyers | "Doing the paper drive this weekend. Anything you wanna donate?" | Subtle |
| Day +2 | Elga in passing | "Oh, the house is just SO full of Tony's papers. He never throws anything out." | Medium — chains Elga + papers |
| Day +3 | Friend (returning encounter) | "Hey, I gotta go put on this dumb uniform and walk all over the neighborhood collecting old papers. People are nice though — they pile up so much junk." | Medium — names the uniform |
| Day +4 | Mom (Kay) at breakfast | "You boys in scouts this year? I saw the flyer. I always liked Mrs. Cole's son in his little uniform — people just hand them whatever they want." | Medium — confirms uniform = access |
| Day +5 | Friend (third encounter) | "Last day of the drive's Saturday. Wish I had a backup — my mom's making me do my whole route alone. You wanna help? Actually no, you'd need the uniform." | Obvious — friend almost says it |
| Day +7 | Danny via comms (if comms unlocked) | *"Mark — did you ever do the Boy Scout paper drive? Mrs. Vinski up the street used to give us a STACK of old magazines every year. Found all kinds of stuff in there."* | Bail-out — Danny names the mechanic |
| Day +10 | Friend (last resort) | *"Dude, just borrow the uniform. I'll lend it to you if you [trade item]. Take my route Saturday. I wanna sleep in."* | Near-explicit — friend offers the deal directly |

**Stop-condition:** Once player borrows uniform, all hints stop firing.
