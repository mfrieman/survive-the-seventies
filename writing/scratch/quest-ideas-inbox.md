## Idea Q-IDEA-01 — Help a friend with their paper route

**Mark's pitch:** Kids had paper routes — *awful* in two parts. (1) delivering papers every day, (2) collecting the money. Collecting was the worst — people couldn't always pay. *"My friend had a paper route and I would go with him to get the money and it was a pain."* Concept: Mark **helps a friend** for a single day (delivery OR collection).

**What's gold:**
- "Two parts of a paper route" is a perfect era-teaching beat — modern players have no idea that kids physically had to **collect the cash** door-to-door.
- The discomfort of collecting from someone who can't pay is *Disco-Elysium-shaped* comedy of social distance.
- "Helping a friend" is the no-stealing-friendly framing — Mark doesn't have his own route, he's helping out.

**Mark Test risk:** Could be boring as a recurring grind. Solved by making it a **one-day contained quest**, not a daily loop.

**Throughline fit:**
- Cash IN: small ($1–2)
- Trade item: a free paper (could matter for a quest where Mark needs a specific newspaper article — Bicentennial coverage, classifieds, etc.)
- NPC: justifies a "kid with paper route" friend AND introduces 2–3 "customer door" mini-NPCs (older lady on fixed income, busy dad never home, the deadbeat house). Each one is one-line characterization.

**Status:** **LIKELY.** Want this in. Best as a one-shot quest with a callback (the friend mentions paper-route stories in later scenes).

## Idea Q-IDEA-02 — Dumpster dive for yard sale items

**Mark's pitch:** *"Maybe there is a quest to go with someone to a junk yard or dumpster diving (something we did) to get yard sale items. This way it would be a contained quest and the items we found would be specifically for the yard sale so there would be no question as to if this item could be used later."*

**What's gold:** This is **the most Mark-Test-aware idea in this dump.** Mark is *himself* solving the hoarding-anxiety problem we identified for the Tear system — by framing the items as quest-specific (only useful for the yard sale, no FOMO about saving them for something else).

Also: dumpster diving by store type ("the appliance shop dumpster has cords and knobs, the bakery dumpster has cardboard, the drugstore dumpster has empty product boxes") is **era-teaching gold** AND character-revealing for whichever friend takes Mark on this.

**Mark Test risk:** Minimal. The "quest-specific items" framing is already the solution.

**Throughline fit:**
- Cash IN: medium ($5–15 from yard sale) — meaningful pinch-point relief
- Trade item: yes — random found items could include trade-worthy goods (a working transistor radio, a deck of cards) that connect to OTHER component quests
- NPC: justifies the friend who takes Mark dumpster-diving — this could be the **paper-route friend (Q-IDEA-01)** or a different one (the "older kid who knows the routes" archetype)
- Locations: requires Zone 1/2 to have known dumpster locations behind specific store types — feeds the locations.md design when Mark dumps his ~20-place list

**Status:** **LIKELY.** Excellent fit. Pair with Q-IDEA-03 (yard sale itself).

## Idea Q-IDEA-04 — Tony's trash (the Playboy magazine source)

**Mark's pitch:** *"Mostly true story."* There was a guy named **Tony**, ~45, single, lived with his old mother **Elga**. Sat on the front lawn in a crappy 70s lawn chair, just small shorts, no shirt, barefoot, reading Playboy magazines. Some women complained but it was funny. Mark's pitch: search Tony's trash to get a Playboy as a kid-economy trade item.

**What's gold:**
- *This is the most Carlin/Pryor/Cheech&Chong-energy beat in the whole dump.* Period-perfect, character-rich, pre-PC in the way the locked tone rules allow (the era is the source of the edge, Mark VO is the conscience).
- Tony + Elga = a fully-realized minor NPC household for almost zero cost.
- Solves the "where do kids get a Playboy" problem **without** Mark stealing — he literally goes through trash put out at the curb (not theft per the locked rule).
- Playboy as **kid-economy trade currency** is era-accurate and high-value — could be a key item for trading for one of the harder-to-get toy components.
- The Threes-Company / Married-with-Children sitcom DNA is exactly the comedic lineage.

**Mark Test risk:** Tonal — needs to be handled per the tone rules (no slurs, Mark VO recoil, NPCs can say things Mark won't say). Should be fine because Tony is a benign weirdo, not punching down.

**Need to rename:** "Playboy" is a real trademark. Need parody name. Quick options:
- **Playmate** (too close)
- **Centerfold** (descriptive, no trademark, period-y)
- **Penthouse** (also real)
- **Stag** (real 70s mag but lesser-known)
- **Stallion** (parody, period-y)
- **Sundown** (could work as a parody men's mag name)

**Throughline fit:**
- Cash IN: trade-only, not cash
- Trade item: ✅ — Playboy-equivalent is a high-value kid-economy trade. Could be required for one of the "harder" toy components (a kid who has the lawn dart tip and ONLY trades for a Playboy).
- NPC: ✅ Tony + Elga as a Zone 1/2 household. Could become a recurring background presence.
- Locations: Tony's front lawn becomes a known landmark.

**Status:** **LIKELY — strongly recommend in.** Want to lock the parody name with Mark.

## Idea Q-IDEA-06 — Build a firework for the pyromaniac kid

**Mark's pitch:** *True story.* Mark made his own fireworks from cap rolls, match heads, and ground-up sparklers. Rolled in newspaper, wrapped in tape, softball-sized. Lit it on his dad's new picnic table. **It was a dud and burned the picnic table.** Dad wasn't even mad — thought it was hilarious and built a better picnic table.

Concept: a **pyromaniac NPC kid** asks Mark to build him a homemade firework for a few dollars. The kid loves it even though it's a dud.

**What's gold:**
- *This is exactly the era-comedy register we want.* Disco-Elysium energy on the dad reaction ("dad wasn't even mad" is the comedy of a generation that was used to kids almost killing themselves).
- Builds on Mark's **matchbook collection** (something kids actually did — picking up matchbooks from the ground) — gives the matchbook collection a USE.
- The pyromaniac kid is a great recurring NPC archetype — could appear in other beats.
- "Mark Cole, the kid from 2027, is now solemnly assembling a homemade explosive in a 1976 garage" is a *killer* Mark VO moment.

**Mark Test risk:** Could be too dark depending on framing. Solve: the firework is a *dud,* nobody gets hurt, the pyro kid loves the spectacle anyway. Played for comedy of inadequacy.

**Throughline fit:**
- Cash IN: small-medium ($3–8 depending on how good the firework is)
- Trade item: ✅ — the kid has something Mark needs (could be a tin-can-and-string for a toy component, a specific marble, etc.). Bonus: the firework build USES collected items (matchbooks, sparklers, cap rolls) — Mark's hoarding of "junk" finally pays off.
- NPC: justifies a "pyromaniac kid" NPC, classic block character
- Era teach: matchbook collection, kids-and-fire culture, dad-not-mad-because-1976
- **MIGHT BE A MACHINE COMPONENT PROVIDER:** worth checking if any of the 10 toy components could naturally come from this kid as the trade payment for the firework

**Status:** **LIKELY — strongly recommend in.** Best when paired with matchbook-collection ambient mechanic.

(like Elga) rather than a one-visit vending machine. Q-003 already yields the dead amp (Marshall transformer +

want to lose total content (5–7 hr target). Solution: keep the realistic single salvage, and bank a *new

**Candidate situations (pick one when promoting):**

- **Recover "borrowed" gear** — a rival kid/older teen "borrowed" their guitar/mic and won't give it back; non-confrontational recovery (wit/trade, not combat).

- **Cut a demo** — sneak/charm time at the radio station to record a demo (ties to a radio-DJ NPC = rumor/clue delivery, per the That-'70s-Show mine).

verb than Q-003 (find-a-person / recover-an-item / negotiate-access) so it doesn't feel like a repeat.

**Throughline fit:**
- Machine part: ❓ TBD — could yield a *different* Signal/Vapor component OR a trade currency. Only promote if

  allow, this is the natural place to *add* a part (Mark: "we can always add another part if the story lacks").
- NPC: reuses Tony Caruso + band (no new household cost); may introduce a radio-DJ or rival-kid NPC.
- Locations: reuses Caruso garage; may add the Hub / radio station / roller rink (cross-ref That-'70s-Show mine).

**Status:** **IDEA — reserved content slot.** Promote when (a) we know which machine part/trade it advances, and
(b) Act 1–2 pacing wants another band beat. Cross-ref: `quests/Q-003-caruso-band-light-show.md`.

## Idea Q-IDEA-07 — Parent errands (cigarettes from vending, cashing checks)

**Mark's pitch:** Parents sent kids to buy things and gave them small change for doing it. Two specific era beats:
1. **Cigarette vending machines at gas stations** — kids buying smokes for adults. (Another payphone-equivalent: also a coin-return slot to check.)
2. **Cashing checks at stores** — there were no ATMs, fewer banks. Parents sent kids to a store to cash a check.

**What's gold:**
- *Pure era teaching for younger players* — both behaviors are unthinkable now, but completely normal in 1976.
- Cigarette vending = the *"wait, you actually DID that?"* reaction we want for Gen Z players.
- Cashing checks at a store = also unfamiliar to anyone under ~30.
- Adds NEW coin-return locations (cigarette vending machines) to the payphone-route mechanic.

**Mark Test risk:** Sending a kid to buy cigarettes is the kind of beat that requires the locked tone treatment — Mark VO comments on it, doesn't pretend it's normal. *"I am 14 and I just bought my dad's mom a carton of Larks. Nobody asked."*

**Throughline fit:**
- Cash IN: small ($0.25 tip per errand)
- Trade item: parent owes a favor (light relationship currency)
- NPC: each errand is a relationship beat with whichever parent sent Mark (great for the Kay-Mark relationship arc, the Dennis-Mark arc, or the Kowalski-family-trust arc with Iwona's mom)
- Locations: gas station with vending machine, neighborhood store that cashes checks — both add to the Zone 1/2 map

**Status:** **LIKELY.** Multiple small versions of this could be sprinkled throughout the game, each one a 30-second era-teaching beat.

## Q-IDEA-11 — The VW Bus Hippies (TRAVELING MERCHANT FACTION) ⭐ NEW 2026-05-29

> **This is not a quest — it's a recurring SYSTEM and a new NPC faction.** Logged here so it
> doesn't get lost; needs its own character/faction doc when we do the character pass.

**The pitch (Mark, 2026-05-29):**
A small group of hippies who live out of a **VW Bus** (already canon as a block vehicle — see
`bible.md`, "Cars on the block: VW Bus (the hippies)"). They are **itinerant** — they roll into
town **every few days**, set up, and **sell items**: flowers, herbs, and other counterculture
goods. Then they leave. They are a *roving shop with legs*, not a fixed location.

**Why this is gold (systemic, not just flavor):**
- **Solves the "where do I buy soft/organic ingredients" problem.** They're the natural source for
  herbs/botanicals that a 14-year-old can't get at the hardware store or grocery — feeds the
  **Vapor pathway** (herbal censer ingredients, brew flavor) and possibly Amber-adjacent oddities.
- **Creates scarcity/timing tension for free.** Because they only show up every few days, certain
  goods are **time-gated** — the player learns their rough schedule and plans around it. That's a
  soft clock that makes the world feel alive WITHOUT a hard fail timer.
- **Pairs with DENISE for the SECOND PATH (Vapor).** Mark: "This is going to work with Denise for
  the second path." Denise (spiritual/folklore neighbor, sourced the original amber) is the
  *knowledge* node; the hippies are the *supply* node. Denise tells you WHAT you need and what it
  means; the hippies are WHERE some of it comes from. Clean division: lore vs. logistics.
- **Tone-perfect for the era and the satire.** Friendly, vague, harmless burnout energy. Comedy
  from the gap between what they think they're selling (enlightenment, good vibes) and what Mark
  actually needs it for (feeding a soul-transfer machine).

**Throughline fit:**
- Machine part / ingredient: **Vapor pathway** botanicals/herbs (censer + brew flavor tier);
  possible one-off oddity that Denise identifies. NOT a Signal-pathway hard part — they're a
  *consumable/ingredient* supplier more than a single-part source.
- NPC: new faction (2–3 named hippies, or 1 named "face" + the bus). Low household cost because
  they're not a fixed family — they share one location asset (the bus) and appear/disappear.
- Locations: the bus parks somewhere recurring (the Hub / 7-Eleven lot / park edge). Mobile asset.

**Open design questions (for the character pass):**
- How does the player learn their schedule? (Denise tip? a flyer? word of mouth?)
- Do they barter (kid-economy trade) or take cash? Barter fits the vibe and the no-money-for-kids reality.
- Are they comic-relief only, or do they carry one quiet "the 60s ended and left these guys behind" beat?
- One named contact vs. an ensemble.

**Status:** **CANON-INTENT — needs a faction/character doc.** Mark has locked the concept; the
*details* are open. Promote individual transactions into Vapor-pathway quests as needed.
